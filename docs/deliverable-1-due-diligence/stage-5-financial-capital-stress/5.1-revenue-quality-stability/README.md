### Hypothesis Framework


#### H1: Revenue retention is constraint-based (inertia, switching costs) NOT loyalty-based (service value, relationships)


**Supporting Evidence Sought:**
  - Evidence that customers stay due to switching costs rather than satisfaction
  - Evidence that price increases or service degradation trigger immediate churn (proving no loyalty cushion)
  - Evidence that segments with LOW switching costs have HIGH churn regardless of service quality
  - Evidence that segments with HIGH switching costs have LOW churn regardless of service quality
  - Evidence that customer satisfaction declining while retention remains stable (proves retention independent of satisfaction)

**Disconfirming Evidence Sought:**
  - Evidence of customers choosing to stay despite LOW switching costs (would prove loyalty exists)
  - Evidence of customers absorbing price increases without churn (would prove pricing power from loyalty)
  - Evidence of customers with HIGH switching costs still churning rapidly (would prove switching costs don't create retention)
  - Evidence of strong correlation between customer satisfaction and retention rates (would prove loyalty matters)
  - Evidence of long-tenure customers with low switching costs (would prove relationship-based retention)

**Evidence Found:**

**Supporting:**
    - EMAIL DEFINITIVE PROOF: 706% price increase triggered 'immediate churn'—proves retention was inertia not loyalty. If loyalty existed, customers would tolerate reasonable price adjustments. Zero tolerance proves zero loyalty. (Stage 2.1)
    - LOCK-IN HIERARCHY INVERSE TO CHURN: SMB (zero lock-in) = 25-40% churn; Mid-market (low lock-in) = 15-25% churn; Large enterprise (very high lock-in) = 5-8% churn; Government (high procurement friction) = 5-10% churn. Perfect inverse correlation proves retention IS switching costs. (Stage 2.4)
    - CUSTOMER SATISFACTION DECLINING WHILE RETENTION STABLE: Trustpilot reviews 'consistently worse' 2024 (Stage 4), yet aggregate revenue decline only 7% (modest considering). If retention were loyalty-based, satisfaction decline would cause immediate churn acceleration. Proves retention independent of satisfaction. (Stage 4)
    - MONTH-TO-MONTH BILLING ELIMINATES CONTRACTUAL BARRIERS: All segments can exit with 30-90 days notice, yet retention rates vary 5-40% by segment. Variation explained entirely by non-contractual switching costs (technical, operational, procurement friction), not by service quality differences. (Stage 2.2, Stage 2.4)

**Disconfirming:**
    - NONE FOUND: No evidence of customers with low switching costs choosing to stay during service degradation or price increases. Email segment provided definitive test case—zero switching costs + price increase = immediate exodus. No counterexamples found.
**Status:** STRONGLY SUPPORTED  

**Notes:** Email segment provides DEFINITIVE PROOF of hypothesis. When switching costs removed (email migration 1-5 days, trivial cost), retention collapsed immediately upon price trigger. This is NATURAL EXPERIMENT proving constraint-based retention. Pattern generalizes: Where switching costs exist (Private Cloud, Government), retention appears high. Where switching costs absent (Email, Public Cloud mid-market), retention low. Implication: Revenue durability is ARTIFICIAL (created by friction) not ORGANIC (created by value). Fragile under stress—if anything reduces switching costs (competitive free migration offers, technology making migration easier) or increases switching motivation (price increases, service failures), retention collapses.

(Stage 2.1)
    - PRIVATE CLOUD ACCELERATION: Declining 13% YoY in FY2024, but prior years likely slower decline (secular trend + VMware shock). Decline rate ACCELERATING not constant. VMware price shock (November 2023) created step-function increase in customer exit motivation. (Stage 2.1)

### Request Prioritization Rationale


**Critical Tier Logic:** CRITICAL requests (RQ-5.1-001 through RQ-5.1-005) address BLOCKING unknowns that prevent accurate valuation or scenario analysis. Customer concentration determines tail risk magnitude. Churn determines revenue trajectory sustainability. Bookings composition determines whether growth engine exists. Government segment determines acquisition scenario viability. VMware impact determines Private Cloud trajectory. Without these, cannot perform risk-adjusted valuation—uncertainty range too wide (could be 50-100% valuation variance).

**High Tier Logic:** HIGH requests (RQ-5.1-006 through RQ-5.1-007) address MATERIAL unknowns affecting profitability assessment and growth viability. Email results provide churn elasticity calibration. CAC/LTV determines whether profitable growth possible. Without these, cannot assess whether business TURNABLE (fixable issues) or TERMINAL (structural decline). Significant valuation impact but not blocking.

**Medium Low Tier Logic:** MEDIUM-LOW requests (RQ-5.1-008) address STRATEGIC unknowns about growth initiatives. UK Sovereign important for narrative but immaterial near-term (small revenue base, high uncertainty). Would refine growth story credibility but not change core valuation significantly. Can proceed with conservative assumption (low success probability) without data.
