# Stage 4: Operating Model Execution


## Stage Orientation

> **Stage 4 Orientation - Operating Model, Organizational Reality, and Execution Physics**
> Status: `APPROVED`


### Stage 4 Intent

**Primary Purpose:** Determine how work actually flows, who controls outcomes, and where execution is structurally constrained  
**Core Question:** If we try to change anything material, where does the organization resist, fracture, or silently refuse?  
**Standard:** M&A operational diligence + post-close operability  
**Output Type:** Execution reality map, not org diagrams  

**Discovery Domains:**
  - Decision authority vs reporting structure - who actually decides, who can veto
  - End-to-end operating model and handoff reality - where work degrades
  - Global delivery model and geographic fragmentation - where time zones and geopolitics create execution constraints
  - Talent systems and incentive alignment - whether people are rewarded for executing the business model
  - Operating model scalability and change absorption capacity - what breaks under load or change
  - Operating model coherence and internal tension - where contradictions persist

**Why Stage 4 Precedes Others:**
  - Technology assessment is meaningless if the organization cannot execute coordinated change
  - Data and AI strategies fail if decision authority is fragmented or obscured
  - Risk mitigation plans are fictional if incentives punish those who raise issues
  - Financial projections based on growth assume scalability that may not exist
  - Integration planning requires understanding where the organization will resist, not just what the org chart shows

**Truths Not Fixable Retroactively:**
  - Missed shadow veto points - post-close plans stall indefinitely when unidentified actors block execution
  - Undetected handoff failures - margin erosion or quality degradation cannot be reversed after close
  - Geographic fragility missed - geopolitical events or time-zone physics create unrecoverable execution gaps
  - Incentive misalignment ignored - talent attrition post-close destroys institutional knowledge
  - Change capacity misjudged - transformation plans collapse under organizational load
  - Operating model contradictions assumed away - post-close chaos when workarounds fail

**Prohibited Activities:**
  - Create org charts or reporting structure diagrams without decision power mapping

  - Design post-close integration plans or organizational restructuring
  - Recommend leadership changes or talent upgrades
  - Propose incentive redesign or compensation changes
  - Prescribe operating model improvements or efficiency initiatives

**Prohibited Analysis Types:**
  - Leadership quality assessments divorced from execution outcomes
  - Best practice comparisons without evidence of execution reality
  - Process maturity models without handoff failure mapping
  - Organizational health scores without scalability testing
  - Culture characterizations without incentive structure evidence

**Boundary Violations:**
  - Using 'alignment' without identifying who blocks what
  - Attributing execution failures solely to 'process' or 'culture'
  - Treating global delivery as interchangeable labor without time-zone or geopolitical analysis
  - Assuming incentives are aligned because management intends them to be
  - Labeling the operating model 'coherent' without surfacing internal tensions
  - Inferring decision authority solely from job titles
  - Describing scalability without testing failure modes

### Stage 4 Failure Conditions


**Invalidation Triggers:**


##### Authority Inferred from Titles


**Description:** Decision power mapped from org chart positions rather than observed decision outcomes; formal authority assumed to equal real authority

##### Execution Drag Attributed to Culture


**Description:** Handoff failures, delays, or quality loss blamed on 'culture' or 'process' without structural analysis of accountability gaps or incentive misalignment

##### Geographic Interchangeability Assumed


**Description:** Global delivery treated as cost arbitrage without analyzing time-zone coordination failures, geopolitical risk, or single-point-of-failure concentration

##### Incentive Alignment Assumed


**Description:** Compensation structures not analyzed for unintended behaviors; assumption that management intent equals actual incentive effects

##### Scalability Untested


**Description:** Growth capacity assumed rather than tested against historical volume/complexity stress points; heroics mistaken for sustainable execution

##### Contradictions Ignored


**Description:** Operating model presented as coherent when centralization/decentralization tensions, cost/quality tradeoffs, or conflicting mandates are visible but unexplored

**False Completeness Indicators:**
  - Org charts presented without decision veto mapping
  - Process flows shown without handoff failure analysis
  - Geographic footprint described without criticality or risk assessment
  - Incentive programs listed without behavioral outcome analysis
  - Scalability claimed without historical stress testing
  - Operating model described without surfacing internal tensions

**Downstream Lies:**
  - Presenting 'alignment' when shadow authority centers exist
  - Claiming 'efficiency' in global delivery without acknowledging time-zone drag
  - Stating 'scalability' when growth historically triggered quality degradation
  - Asserting 'incentive alignment' when sales comp rewards short-term deals that create long-term service problems
  - Describing 'mature processes' when execution relies on heroics and workarounds
  - Characterizing operating model as 'coherent' when contradictions require leadership intervention to resolve

### Stage 4 Hypothesis Register


**Governance Note:** Per Stage 0.3 requirements: hypotheses must be falsifiable, structural (not performance-based), evidence-based (not narrative-based)

**Hypotheses:**


##### H4.1


**Hypothesis:** Decision authority is fragmented across functional silos with multiple veto points that can stall strategic initiatives without formal rejection
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Initiative failure patterns disclosed in earnings calls or restructuring announcements
    - Committee structures with overlapping authority
    - Leadership commentary about 'alignment challenges' or 'cross-functional coordination'
    - Evidence of escalation forums used to resolve routine decisions

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Clear accountability for end-to-end outcomes in single roles
    - Rapid decision cycles with minimal escalation
    - Successful execution of cross-functional initiatives at scale
    - Org structure with unified P&L accountability

**Falsification Approach:** Analyze disclosed initiative timelines, committee charters, decision escalation paths, and leadership commentary on decision-making speed
**Status:** 🔍 PENDING  

##### H4.2


**Hypothesis:** Execution degrades at handoff points between sales-delivery, delivery-support, or product-engineering due to misaligned incentives or accountability gaps
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Customer complaints about post-sales execution or support quality
    - NPS or CSAT decline patterns tied to specific handoff stages
    - Restructuring efforts focused on 'end-to-end ownership'
    - Disclosed SLA misses or delivery delays

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Consistent service quality metrics across customer lifecycle
    - Unified teams with end-to-end accountability
    - High NPS with no handoff-related complaint patterns
    - Evidence of seamless handoffs without rework

**Falsification Approach:** Map process flows from disclosed descriptions, analyze customer complaint patterns, review SLA/KPI performance, examine reorg rationale for handoff fixes
**Status:** 🔍 PENDING  

##### H4.3


**Hypothesis:** Geographic delivery fragmentation creates time-zone coordination delays, single points of failure, or geopolitical execution risk
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Disclosed delivery locations with critical functions concentrated in single regions
    - Incident response or escalation delays attributed to time-zone coverage gaps
    - Outsourcing or offshoring disclosures without redundancy evidence
    - Geopolitical risk disclosures (labor disputes, regulatory changes, sanctions)

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Follow-the-sun delivery model with documented redundancy
    - Multi-region delivery with no critical single points of failure
    - Evidence of seamless global coordination
    - No disclosed geopolitical or labor disruptions affecting operations

**Falsification Approach:** Map disclosed delivery locations against critical function requirements, analyze incident response capabilities, review geopolitical risk factors
**Status:** 🔍 PENDING  

##### H4.4


**Hypothesis:** Sales or operational incentives reward short-term outcomes that create long-term execution problems, margin erosion, or customer churn
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Sales compensation heavily weighted to bookings vs. renewal or profitability
    - Evidence of deal discounting pressure or margin erosion post-sale
    - Customer churn patterns tied to overselling or misaligned expectations
    - Disclosed incentive structures misaligned with stated strategic priorities

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Compensation tied to customer lifetime value, retention, or profitability
    - Stable margins without post-sale erosion
    - High renewal rates with no overselling evidence
    - Aligned incentives across sales, delivery, and support

**Falsification Approach:** Analyze disclosed compensation structures, margin trends post-sale, churn patterns, customer complaint themes, sales cycle vs. delivery execution gaps
**Status:** 🔍 PENDING  

##### H4.5


**Hypothesis:** Operating model cannot scale without degradation—historical growth periods correlate with service quality decline, margin pressure, or operational chaos
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Historical periods of rapid growth followed by quality metric declines
    - Restructuring or 'focus' initiatives after expansion attempts
    - Disclosed capacity constraints or delivery delays during growth
    - Leadership commentary about 'stabilization' or 'operational rigor' post-growth

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Consistent quality metrics during historical growth periods
    - Evidence of scalable processes without heroics
    - No restructuring or 'focus' initiatives post-growth
    - Margin expansion with volume growth

**Falsification Approach:** Correlate revenue growth periods with service quality metrics, margin trends, restructuring timing, and leadership commentary on operational challenges
**Status:** 🔍 PENDING  

##### H4.6


**Hypothesis:** Operating model contains unresolved contradictions (centralized/decentralized, cost/quality, speed/control) that persist through workarounds and leadership intervention
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sources:**
    - Evidence of centralization/decentralization oscillation in restructuring history

    - Disclosed efficiency initiatives followed by quality problems
    - Matrix structures or dual reporting without clear accountability

**Disconfirming Evidence Sources:**
    - Stable operating model without structural reorganizations
    - Clear authority and accountability without matrix ambiguity
    - No disclosed tradeoffs between speed, cost, and quality
    - Consistent execution without contradictory mandates

**Falsification Approach:** Analyze restructuring frequency and rationale, leadership commentary on organizational tension, evidence of conflicting mandates or competing priorities
