### Sub Stage

4.1

### Authority Mismatches

**Decision Type:** Business Unit Performance & Strategic Direction  

**Where Authority Differs From Accountability:** Business Unit Presidents (Private Cloud, Public Cloud) have title suggesting P&L accountability, but lack decision authority over pricing, resource allocation, or cross-functional issue resolution. Evidence: Private Cloud President Lillie departed Jan 2025, CEO assumed role directly rather than replacing—indicates role lacked sufficient authority to be viable. Public Cloud President not named in public disclosures—suggests low organizational authority.

**Execution Drag Created:** BU Presidents cannot resolve cross-functional conflicts (sales vs delivery, profitability vs growth) within their units. Issues escalate to CEO, creating bottleneck. BU strategy subordinate to corporate strategy with no evidence of autonomous BU decision-making. Customer escalations within BU cannot be resolved at BU level (per customer complaints about inability to reach senior management). Result: BU structure creates reporting clarity but not decision autonomy—CEO must adjudicate BU-level issues, limiting scalability.
**Claim Type:** `INFERENCE`  

**Evidence Sources:**
  - Brian Lillie departure Jan 2025 (Private Cloud President)
  - CEO Amar Maletira assumed interim Private Cloud leadership (no replacement appointed)
  - Public Cloud President not identified in earnings calls or SEC filings
  - Customer complaints: cannot escalate to senior management within BU
  - CEO statements in earnings calls articulate strategy for both BUs—no BU President strategy ownership
