### Accountability Voids

**Void:** Deal profitability—accountability split between sales (booking) and delivery (execution) with no unified owner  

**Why Void Exists:** Sales has authority to book deals without profitability validation (Stage 4.1). Delivery has accountability for margin execution without authority to reject unprofitable deals (Stage 4.1). No deal review board or cross-functional governance to assess profitability before signature (Stage 4.1). Accountability split creates void—sales blamed for low bookings, delivery blamed for low margins, but neither accountable for profitable deal flow (the actual business outcome).

**Who Should Be Accountable:** UNCLEAR from organizational structure. Options: (1) Deal review board with sales/delivery/finance representation and go/no-go authority, (2) Chief Revenue Officer with authority over both sales and delivery, (3) BU Presidents with authority to approve deals within BU P&L accountability. Currently: CEO by default (adjudicates conflicts) but no formal accountability—CEO reactive not proactive.

**Consequences Of Void:** Margin erosion: Private Cloud -13%, Public Cloud -3% FY2024. Delivery capacity consumed executing unprofitable deals. Customer execution failures from under-resourced unprofitable accounts. CEO forced to intervene reactively (Q4 2024 walk away policy) after severe deterioration. No proactive deal discipline—pattern persists until crisis forces recognition. Sales refresh Q3 2024 treats symptom (personnel) not cause (accountability void).

**Why Void Persists:** Fixing void requires authority reallocation—either sales loses booking autonomy or delivery gains veto authority. Authority reallocation creates conflict: Sales argues veto slows deal closure (competitive disadvantage), delivery argues booking autonomy destroys margins (financial unsustainability). CEO hasn't resolved conflict—2023 reorganization created BU structure but didn't reallocate sales/delivery authority. Walk away policy Q4 2024 is CEO-level workaround not structural fix—when CEO bandwidth exhausted, void reopens.

**Touch Test:** If deal profitability accountability unified (single owner with authority and accountability), would margins improve? HYPOTHESIS: Yes—unified owner would conduct profitability validation pre-signature, reject unprofitable deals proactively, negotiate pricing reflecting true cost. Evidence: Walk away policy Q4 2024 demonstrates deals can be rejected when profitability prioritized—but required CEO intervention (not scalable). Healthcare deal $100M+ TCV required CEO involvement—indicates major deal accountability defaults to CEO. Suggests void persists because no one below CEO has unified authority.
**Claim Type:** `INFERENCE`  

**Evidence Sources:**
  - Stage 4.1: Sales has booking authority, delivery has margin accountability
  - Stage 4.1: No deal review board
  - Margin erosion: Private Cloud -13%, Public Cloud -3%
  - Q4 2024: CEO walk away policy (reactive)
  - Q3 2024: Sales refresh
  - Healthcare deal: CEO involvement $100M+ TCV
  - 2023 reorganization: BU structure created but authority gap persists

**Who Should Be Accountable:** UNCLEAR from organizational structure. Options: (1) CRO with proactive risk governance authority and budget, (2) CISO with authority beyond reactive security to proactive risk investment, (3) COO with accountability for operational stability not just incident response. Currently: Operations by default but measured reactively. Technical debt accumulation as proactive infrastructure investment deferred.

Proactive investment delivers uncertain future benefit (prevented incidents) vs certain current cost (budget allocation). No executive advocate for proactive risk—operations focused on response, finance focused on cost control, CEO focused on immediate crisis resolution.

Quarterly/annual quotas create urgency to book deals even if marginal/unprofitable—end-of-period gaming. Career progression rewards top bookings—sales reps competing on volume not value.

**Systemic Cost:** Delivery capacity consumed executing unprofitable deals—less capacity for profitable customers. Margin destruction: Private Cloud -13%, Public Cloud -3% FY2024. Customer execution failures from under-resourced unprofitable accounts—churn risk. Revenue growth sacrificed for profitability—Q4 2024 walk away policy rejects renewals, FY2024 -7% revenue decline. Delivery team morale degradation—forced to execute impossible commitments. Competitive position erodes—cannot invest in differentiation when margins consumed by unprofitable deals.

**Why Drag Persists:** Sales organization resists profitability accountability—argues veto authority slows deal closure (competitive disadvantage). Sales management evaluated on team bookings—management rational to defend sales autonomy. Sales refresh Q3 2024 replaced personnel but not incentive structure—treats symptom not cause. Walk away policy Q4 2024 reactive (CEO intervention after crisis) not proactive (sales incentive realignment). Changing incentive structure complex: Requires delivered profitability measurement by deal (data infrastructure may not exist), creates conflict over attribution when deals modified post-signature, reduces sales compensation in short term (retention risk).
