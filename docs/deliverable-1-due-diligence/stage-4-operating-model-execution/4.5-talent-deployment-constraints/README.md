### Hypothesis Framework


#### System constraints neutralize talent capability—adding capable individuals doesn't materially improve execution because structural gaps require heroics regardless of individual competence

**Hypothesis Id:** H4.5-A  
**Type:** STRUCTURAL  

**Supporting Evidence Sought:**
  - Execution patterns persisting across personnel changes (three CEOs—bottleneck recreates, sales refresh—margin erosion continues)
  - Capable talent deployed in heroic mode rather than effective mode (support coordinating dozens of engineers, operations sustaining multi-month crises)
  - Authority-accountability mismatches forcing heroics (support lacks authority, delivery lacks veto, BU Presidents lack cross-functional authority)
  - System design requiring personal intervention (CEO coordination, support informal networks, operations crisis response)

**Disconfirming Evidence Sought:**
  - Personnel additions or changes materially improving execution without structural reform
  - Talented individuals changing outcomes despite structural constraints
  - Execution failures resolved through hiring rather than structural reform
  - Examples of talent capability overcoming system constraints without heroics

**Evidence Found:**

**Supporting:**
    - Three CEOs (Jones, Maletira, Kandiah)—bottleneck pattern recreates regardless of individual, indicates structural not individual constraint
    - Sales refresh Q3 2024—margin erosion continues (Private Cloud -13%, Public Cloud -3%), indicates incentive structure not personnel issue
    - Support coordinating 'dozens of engineers' informally—capable talent deployed heroically not effectively due to authority gap
    - Operations sustaining multi-month crises reactively—capable incident response but structural prevention gap forces heroics
    - Authority gaps persist: No VP Support (Stage 4.1), no operating committee (Stage 4.1), no deal review board (Stage 4.1)
    - Walk away policy Q4 2024 requires CEO enforcement—indicates sales talent cannot self-regulate without structural support

**Disconfirming:**
    - No evidence of personnel changes resolving execution failures without structural reform
    - No evidence of talented individuals overcoming structural constraints sustainably (only heroic temporary workarounds)
    - No examples of hiring resolving systemic execution issues
**Status:** ✅ SUPPORTED  

**Notes:** Multiple execution patterns demonstrate system constraints defeating talent capability. Three CEOs inherit identical coordination bottleneck—indicates structural issue not CEO capability deficit. Sales refresh doesn't resolve margin erosion—indicates incentive/authority structure not sales personnel. Support staff coordinate heroically because authority gap structural—more talented support staff would face same authority constraint. Operations respond to incidents heroically because prevention structurally unfunded—more talented operations staff would inherit same reactive-only posture. Hypothesis: Adding talented individuals addresses symptom (execution failure) not cause (structural constraint). System reform necessary—talent optimization insufficient.
