**Data Domain:** Deal profitability accuracy—CRM opportunity value vs actual margin reality  

**Formal Owner:** FRAGMENTED WITHOUT CLEAR STEWARDSHIP TITLE. Sales formally owns CRM data entry and opportunity qualification (controls source system Stage 7.2 transformation T1). Finance formally owns margin reporting and external guidance (controls aggregation and adjustment Stage 7.2). No formal 'deal profitability data owner' role identified—ownership implied through system control not explicit stewardship assignment.

**De Facto Owner:** CEO REACTIVE ARBITER. CEO intervenes when profitability gap severe (walk away policy Q4 2024, sales refresh Q3 2024), becomes de facto owner through crisis management. CEO doesn't own CRM methodology or margin reporting systems, but owns filtering decisions—de facto ownership through override authority not systematic stewardship. Pre-Q4 2024, domain effectively ORPHANED—sales entered optimistic data, delivery discovered cost reality, finance reported aggregate, but no single owner accountable for CRM-profitability alignment until CEO forced to intervene.

**Fix Authority:** DISTRIBUTED WITHOUT COORDINATION. Sales has authority to change CRM qualification criteria (can tighten entry standards, require profitability validation before opportunity creation), but sales refresh Q3 2024 changed personnel not methodology—authority not exercised. Finance has authority to adjust aggregation methodology or discount CRM pipeline more conservatively, but adjustment methodology opaque (Stage 7.2 uncertainty finance adjustment undocumented). CEO has authority to override (walk away policy filters deals sales accepted), but CEO lacks bandwidth to systematically validate all opportunities (Stage 4.2 CEO bottleneck)—override selective not comprehensive. Delivery lacks authority—discovers cost reality post-signature but cannot reject deals or change CRM qualification standards (authority-accountability gap Stage 4.1).

**Blame Pattern:** BLAME CASCADES THROUGH FUNCTIONS, LANDS ON EXECUTION. First blamed: SALES for pipeline optimism (sales refresh Q3 2024 personnel changes imply sales performance/judgment blamed). Second blamed: DELIVERY for margin erosion (delivery absorbs negative margins from unprofitable deals Stage 7.3 error tolerance, delivery 'failed to execute profitably' narrative possible even when deal unprofitable from entry). Third blamed (if escalates): CEO for resource allocation based on inflated pipeline. NEVER BLAMED: Finance (reports aggregate margins accurately even if operationally uninformed, external reporting protected by audit/adjustment Stage 7.3 selective enforcement). CRM SYSTEM not blamed—inaccuracy attributed to human judgment (sales optimism, delivery execution) not systematic methodology failure. Blame pattern enables: Sales optimism continues (methodology unchanged post-refresh, new personnel may repeat pattern), delivery accepts unprofitable deals (no deal rejection authority), CEO firefights reactively (walk away policy treats symptoms not causes).

**Organizational Risk:** BLAME MISALLOCATION PREVENTS SYSTEMATIC FIX. If sales blamed for individual judgment/performance, solution is personnel change (sales refresh Q3 2024)—but if root cause is incentive structure (bookings-based compensation Stage 4.4) or methodology absence (no profitability validation at entry), personnel change insufficient and pattern recurs. If delivery blamed for execution cost, solution is delivery efficiency improvement—but if root cause is CRM entry optimism (deals unprofitable from contract signature), delivery efficiency cannot compensate. Blame landing on execution (sales personnel, delivery cost management) not methodology (CRM validation absence, incentive misalignment) prevents structural fix. CEO reactive intervention (walk away policy) bypasses systematic accountability—CEO becomes permanent filter/arbiter, doesn't reassign ownership or build governance preventing need for CEO intervention. M&A risk: Acquirer discovers accountability diffusion—when asking 'who owns deal profitability accuracy?', answer ambiguous (sales enters, finance reports, CEO filters, delivery executes, but no single steward), integration requires accountability consolidation or acquirer inherits firefighting pattern.

**Touch Test What Breaks If Accountability Enforced:** ENFORCING CLEAR ACCOUNTABILITY = ASSIGN SINGLE OWNER FOR CRM-PROFITABILITY ALIGNMENT WITH AUTHORITY AND CONSEQUENCE. If SALES made accountable: Sales compensation must penalize forecast errors not just reward bookings (Stage 4.4 incentive realignment required), sales cycle lengthens (profitability validation before CRM entry adds time), sales pipeline volume declines (unprofitable opportunities rejected at entry not post-signature), sales turnover may increase (accountability for margin accuracy harder than bookings volume). If DELIVERY made accountable: Delivery gains deal rejection authority (can refuse unprofitable deals sales signed), sales-delivery conflict escalates (delivery rejects deals sales counted as bookings), revenue declines (delivery rejects more conservatively than CEO walk away policy), sales claims delivery 'blocking growth.' If FINANCE made accountable: Finance must validate operational data accuracy not just report aggregates, finance bandwidth increases (operational data quality monitoring beyond financial reporting audit scope Stage 7.3 selective enforcement), finance-operations conflict increases (finance questions operational forecasts creating cross-functional tension). If CEO made accountable: CEO bottleneck worsens (Stage 4.2 CEO already bandwidth-exhausted, systematic deal validation compounds), CEO must build governance infrastructure replacing personal arbitration (operating committee, deal review board Stage 7.1 governance void must be filled), accountability transfer from CEO to governance requires CEO to deprioritize other CEO-only work. CURRENT DIFFUSION preserves each function's local optimization—sales optimizes bookings, delivery executes what sales delivers, finance reports aggregates, CEO intervenes episodically—without enterprise accountability forcing painful tradeoffs.

**Data Domain:** Customer identity and count reconciliation—parallel definitions across CRM, billing, support  

**Formal Owner:** ORPHANED—NO FORMAL STEWARDSHIP ASSIGNMENT. Sales owns CRM customer data (account creation, relationship tracking). Finance owns billing customer data (revenue recognition, active account definition). Support owns engagement customer data (ticket volume, active support relationships). But NO OWNER for customer identity reconciliation or enterprise customer count truth—gap between system-specific ownership and enterprise stewardship. No Chief Data Officer or equivalent role consolidating cross-system customer truth identified. No operating committee or data governance committee owning reconciliation mandate (Stage 7.1 H7.1-B governance void, Stage 4.1 coordination infrastructure absent).

**De Facto Owner:** FINANCE FOR EXTERNAL REPORTING DEFINITION CHOICE, OTHERWISE ORPHANED. Finance chooses which customer definition to report externally (likely billing active accounts for revenue alignment Stage 7.3 customer count error tolerance), becomes de facto owner of external customer count through reporting authority. But internal customer truth remains fragmented—sales uses CRM count for territory/quota planning, support uses engagement count for staffing, no internal reconciliation owner. Customer count disputes escalate to CEO for case-by-case arbitration not systematic resolution (Stage 4.1 no operating committee, Stage 7.3 selective enforcement cross-functional disputes political resolution).

**Fix Authority:** DISTRIBUTED WITHOUT MANDATE. Each function has authority to change own system definition (sales can redefine CRM 'active account', finance can redefine billing 'revenue customer', support can redefine 'engaged customer'), but no function has authority to force enterprise reconciliation or impose single definition across systems. CEO has override authority (can mandate reconciliation, choose enterprise definition), but CEO hasn't exercised—customer count discrepancy tolerated indefinitely (Stage 7.3 customer identity tolerance indefinite). Enterprise data architecture team (if exists) could have technical authority to build reconciliation, but business definition authority (which count is truth?) requires business owner not technical team—business owner absent.

**Blame Pattern:** NO BLAME—ORPHANED DOMAIN. Customer count discrepancies not blamed on any function because no function owns reconciliation. When customer metrics contested (sales claims N customers, finance reports M, support tracks K where N ≠ M ≠ K), dispute treated as 'definitional difference' not 'data failure'—blame avoided through definitional relativism ('both right for their purposes'). If customer count questioned externally (investor asks about discrepancy between earnings call count and other public statements, M&A diligence discovers variance), FINANCE potentially blamed for external reporting choice (why this definition not another?)—but internal discrepancy not finance's fault, finance inherits unreconciled operational data. CEO not blamed—customer count ambiguity hasn't created visible crisis (unlike margin erosion triggering walk away policy), tolerance continues. Blame avoidance enables: Each function continues own definition optimizing locally (sales maximizes CRM count for territory size, finance reports conservative billing count for investor credibility, support tracks engaged count for operational planning), no pressure to reconcile creating enterprise truth.

**Organizational Risk:** ORPHANED ACCOUNTABILITY ALLOWS PERSISTENT AMBIGUITY. Customer count ambiguity undermines: Customer lifetime value calculation (LTV depends on customer count denominator, calculation varies by definition), churn rate accuracy (churn rate = lost customers / total customers, rate varies by which count used as denominator Stage 7.3 customer identity error tolerance), resource allocation (sales territories sized on CRM count, support staffing sized on engagement count, misalignment if counts diverge materially), investor communications (if external count later discovered inaccurate or inconsistently defined, guidance credibility damaged). Orphaned domain persists because reconciliation POLITICALLY EXPENSIVE—forcing single definition requires one function to adopt another's count (sales adopts billing count means CRM count declines, territories shrink, quota achievement rates fall) or requires all functions to adopt new definition (effort to rebuild metrics, historical comparability lost). CEO avoids reconciliation mandate because political cost high (cross-functional conflict) and crisis absent (customer count ambiguity hasn't caused visible failure yet). M&A risk: Acquirer demands customer count reconciliation during diligence, discovers material variance, questions which count accurate, may revalue based on conservative count (if M < N materially, acquirer values on M not N reducing valuation), may require representation about customer count accuracy (representation impossible if counts unreconciled, earnout provisions affected if customer retention calculations depend on denominator definition).

**Touch Test What Breaks If Accountability Enforced:** ENFORCING ACCOUNTABILITY = ASSIGN SINGLE CUSTOMER DATA STEWARD WITH ENTERPRISE RECONCILIATION MANDATE. If SALES made steward: Sales must reconcile CRM with billing and support, CRM 'inactive' accounts identified and removed, CRM count declines (N → M or lower), sales territories shrink (territory size based on account counts), quota achievement rates appear to decline (fewer accounts per rep), sales compensation affected if territory/quota based on inflated CRM count. If FINANCE made steward: Finance must validate operational customer data quality not just report external count, finance bandwidth increases (operational data reconciliation beyond financial reporting scope), finance-sales conflict (finance identifies CRM inactive accounts sales wants to retain for pipeline optics). If SUPPORT made steward: Support engagement definition becomes enterprise standard, inactive contractual customers (CRM/billing) reclassified as 'former customers' if zero engagement, sales/finance customer counts decline to support's engaged count K (if K < M < N), revenue per customer metrics change (if revenue divided by smaller engaged customer base, revenue per customer increases but customer count optics worsen). If NEW ROLE (Chief Data Officer, customer data steward) created: Requires headcount addition (cost) or reassignment (diverts someone from current role), requires cross-functional authority (sales, finance, support must comply with steward's reconciliation mandate), requires CEO backing (steward needs CEO support to enforce compliance when functions resist), governance infrastructure needed (operating committee or data governance committee providing steward forum for dispute resolution Stage 7.1 governance void must be filled). CURRENT ORPHANED STATE preserves each function's definitional autonomy—no forced reconciliation, no political conflict over enterprise truth, no function blamed for ambiguity—at cost of persistent customer metrics unreliability.

**Organizational Risk:** BLAME AVOIDANCE ENABLES MEASUREMENT THEATER. Support metrics (SLA compliance, resolution rates) misleading—exclude executive escalations creating false performance picture. **Data Domain:** Incident frequency tracking and recurrence prevention—response capability vs prevention accountability  

**Formal Owner:** OPERATIONS (INCIDENT RESPONSE AND SYSTEM RELIABILITY). Operations formally owns incident response—detection (MTTD), response (MTTR), resolution, root cause analysis. Operations performance metrics include incident response capability. But INCIDENT PREVENTION ownership fragmented or orphaned: Prevention spans operations (system reliability), delivery (architecture resilience), vendor management (third-party dependency risk Stage 6), security (vulnerability management). No single prevention steward identified. Incident frequency tracking (count over time, recurrence patterns) not systematically measured (Stage 7.3 incident frequency error tolerance), prevention accountability absent despite response accountability present.

**De Facto Owner:** OPERATIONS FOR RESPONSE, PREVENTION ORPHANED. Operations owns incident response in practice—incidents detected, escalated, resolved by operations teams. But incident recurrence prevention orphaned—ransomware multi-month, zero-day Sept 2024 indicate prevention gaps not owned by operations or any function (Stage 7.3 incident frequency tolerance recurrence until visibility risk). When incident recurs, operations responds again (response accountability), prevention failure not blamed on specific owner (prevention accountability absent).

**Fix Authority:** OPERATIONS HAS RESPONSE AUTHORITY, PREVENTION AUTHORITY DIFFUSE. Operations has authority to improve response processes (detection tooling, escalation procedures, resolution playbooks), and exercises this authority (MTTD/MTTR capability strong Stage 7.3). Operations has partial prevention authority (can improve system reliability, reduce incident likelihood through operational excellence), but prevention also requires: Delivery architectural changes (system resilience, redundancy, failure isolation requiring design modifications), vendor management changes (vendor dependency reduction, vendor SLA enforcement, vendor incident accountability Stage 6 vendor dependencies), security team vulnerability management (patch management, security controls, threat prevention). Prevention authority distributed across operations-delivery-vendors-security without single coordinator. CEO could mandate prevention accountability consolidation (assign prevention owner, establish incident review board analyzing recurrence patterns and mandating prevention Stage 7.1 governance void), but CEO hasn't exercised—incident recurrence tolerated while regulatory/customer visibility managed reactively.

**Blame Pattern:** OPERATIONS PRAISED FOR RESPONSE, PREVENTION FAILURE NOT BLAMED. Operations praised when incidents resolved quickly—'strong incident response capability' narrative based on MTTD/MTTR (Stage 7.3 incident data transformation capability-frequency conflation). When incidents recur (ransomware multi-month, zero-day exploitation), recurrence not framed as operations prevention failure but as: (1) External threat sophistication ('advanced persistent threat' framing externalizes blame to attackers not internal prevention gaps), (2) vendor dependency risk ('third-party vulnerability' framing blames vendors not Rackspace prevention Stage 6 vendor dependencies), (3) resource constraints ('prevention requires investment, budget unavailable' framing blames capital limitations Stage 5 $1.3B debt not operations), (4) acceptable operational reality ('some incidents unavoidable in complex systems' framing normalizes recurrence as industry standard not failure). Blame externalization enables: Operations optimizes response capability (controllable, measurable, praised) without prevention accountability (uncontrollable given resource/vendor constraints, measurement absent, blame externalized), prevention investment deprioritized (response cheaper than prevention, response praised while prevention orphaned creates investment bias toward response), incident recurrence continues (prevention gaps unaddressed, ransomware recurs, zero-day exploited, pattern continues).

**Organizational Risk:** BLAME AVOIDANCE ENABLES RESPONSE THEATER WITHOUT PREVENTION. Operations 'strong incident response' claim technically accurate (MTTD/MTTR capability good) but misleading (implies operational stability, contradicted by recurrence Stage 7.3 incident frequency tolerance capability-frequency drift). Customer experience includes incident impact and recurrence—response speed doesn't compensate for stability gaps, customers experience 'operational instability' despite 'strong incident response' claims. Regulatory risk from recurrence—FedRAMP authorization, HIPAA compliance, PCI DSS questioned if incident recurrence pattern indicates inadequate controls (response capability insufficient if prevention absent, compliance auditors may question control effectiveness). Security posture deterioration—if ransomware recurs multi-month without prevention, attackers learn Rackspace vulnerable and targeting continues/accelerates, zero-day Sept 2024 exploitation indicates vulnerability management gaps. Prevention investment ROI unknown—operations cannot justify prevention investment without frequency measurement (Stage 7.3 incident frequency uncertainty), repeated response costs may exceed prevention investment but comparison unavailable without frequency/cost tracking. M&A risk: Acquirer reviews incident history, discovers recurrence patterns (ransomware multi-month, zero-day exploited), questions prevention capability despite response capability strength, may require post-close prevention infrastructure investment (redundancy, vendor dependency reduction Stage 6, security tooling) as integration condition, may adjust valuation for incident-driven stability risk.

**Touch Test What Breaks If Accountability Enforced:** ENFORCING ACCOUNTABILITY = ASSIGN INCIDENT PREVENTION OWNER WITH FREQUENCY TRACKING AND RECURRENCE REDUCTION MANDATE. If OPERATIONS made accountable for prevention: Operations incentive shifts from response speed to incident reduction (Stage 4.4 metrics change from MTTD/MTTR to incident frequency/recurrence rate), operations investment priorities shift (prevention tooling, redundancy, proactive monitoring vs reactive response), operations may demand cross-functional support (delivery architectural changes, vendor SLA enforcement, security patching) to achieve prevention goals creating coordination requirements, operations performance narrative shifts from 'response capability' to 'stability achievement' (harder accountability—recurrence visible, prevention gaps exposed). CURRENT STATE preserves operations response praise (capability strong) without prevention blame (recurrence externalized), prevention investment deprioritization (unmeasured ROI, capital constraints, orphaned accountability creates inaction bias), incident recurrence acceptance (tolerated as operational reality not failure requiring accountability).

**Data Domain:** Multi-jurisdictional operational data aggregation—enterprise metrics vs jurisdiction-specific variance reality  

**Formal Owner:** FINANCE (AGGREGATION METHODOLOGY AND EXTERNAL REPORTING). Finance formally owns aggregation methodology—how jurisdiction-specific metrics (FedRAMP, UK, HIPAA, China, other) combined into enterprise-level reporting for investors, Board, external stakeholders. ### Synthesis Notes

Five material data domains identified as ORPHANED—no clear ownership or accountability. Orphaning patterns: (1) CROSS-SYSTEM GAPS: Customer identity reconciliation orphaned between sales (CRM), finance (billing), support (engagement)—no enterprise steward consolidating. Deal profitability validation orphaned between sales (entry) and delivery (execution)—no bridge owner validating CRM-profitability alignment. (2) CROSS-FUNCTIONAL GAPS: Incident prevention orphaned between operations (reliability), delivery (architecture), vendors (dependencies), security (vulnerabilities)—no prevention coordinator. Support escalation process orphaned between support (ticketing), account management (relationships), executives (resolution)—no escalation steward. (3) OUTCOME MEASUREMENT GAPS: Customer satisfaction measurement orphaned—no owner for enterprise NPS/CSAT despite multiple potential stewards (support, customer success, marketing), measurement absence protects functions from outcome accountability. Orphaning mechanisms: (1) Accountability avoidance—functions avoid ownership of difficult domains (satisfaction measurement exposes activity-outcome divergence, profitability validation delays sales cycle, prevention harder than response), orphaning through non-volunteering. (2) Authority fragmentation—domains requiring cross-functional coordination orphaned when coordination infrastructure absent (no operating committee Stage 4.1, no governance committees Stage 7.1 H7.1-B), CEO bottleneck prevents cross-functional stewardship (Stage 4.2). (3) Measurement absence—domains without measurement lack accountability basis (incident frequency unmeasured means prevention ROI unknown Stage 7.3, satisfaction unmeasured means outcome accountability impossible), measurement gaps enable orphaning. (4) Blame externalization—when domains fail, blame externalized (incident recurrence blamed on threats not prevention, satisfaction decline blamed on market not service) or diffused (customer count contested but no function blamed), lack of blame removes pressure to assign ownership. (5) Political cost avoidance—CEO avoids ownership assignment when politically expensive (customer count reconciliation forces definitional consensus creating conflict, satisfaction measurement exposes multiple functions to accountability tightening), deferral until crisis forces assignment. Orphaning consequences: Persistent problems without resolution (customer metrics unreliable indefinitely, incident recurrence continues, satisfaction deteriorates unaddressed), strategic blindness (churn risk invisible without satisfaction, margin erosion prolonged without profitability validation, prevention gaps unquantified without frequency tracking), M&A valuation uncertainty (orphaned domains exposed during diligence, acquirer demands ownership assignment or discounts for unmanaged risk). Orphaning structural causes: Governance void (Stage 7.1 H7.1-B no committees assigning cross-functional ownership), coordination infrastructure absence (Stage 4.1 no operating committee, no coordinators bridging functional gaps), CEO bottleneck (Stage 4.2 CEO lacks bandwidth for ownership design, reactive crisis management not proactive stewardship assignment), three CEOs three years (Stage 4.5 ownership assignment non-transferable across CEO transitions, orphaning persists institutionally).
