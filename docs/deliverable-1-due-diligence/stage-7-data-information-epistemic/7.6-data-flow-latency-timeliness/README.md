**Claim:** Decisions are made on near-real-time data—executive visibility of operational reality lags events by hours/days not weeks/months, enabling current-state decision-making

  - Incident response visibility immediate per Stage 7.4 mean-time-to-X tracking (hours lag from incident T0 to operations visibility T1, operations sees individual incidents real-time enabling rapid response decisions)
  - CRM entry real-time per systemic failures analysis (sales enters opportunities immediately upon customer engagement, sales management sees pipeline updates real-time for territory management and coaching decisions)

**Evidence Against:**
  - Deal profitability decisions made weeks pre-stabilization per latency map (commitment T2 before delivery validation T3, CEO walk away T5 weeks post-commitment, profitability truth emerges post-decision creating systemic blind spot)
  - Satisfaction visibility months lag or indefinite per latency map measurement paralysis (customer experience T0, Rackspace discovery T3+ through external platforms or T4 churn event, satisfaction decisions made on months-stale or absent data)
  - Margin correction decisions three quarters lag per latency map delayed intervention (Q1-Q3 2024 erosion visible in quarterly closes but intervention Q4 2024, three quarters value destruction while awaiting quarterly data accumulation and decision)

**Hypothesis Status:** DECISIVELY REFUTED

'Near-real-time' claim contradicted by systematic weeks-to-quarters latencies and measurement absence domains (satisfaction, frequency). Decision-data lags not exceptional but pervasive across sales, operations, finance, board, security domains.

**M And A Implications:** Hypothesis refutation reveals decision-making operates on significantly lagged or absent data not near-real-time operational truth. Acquirer discovers material decisions (deal commitments, margin interventions, satisfaction actions, prevention investment, board oversight, forecast commitments) systematically made on stale fictions not current reality.
