## Hypotheses


### Hypothesis Framework


#### H1: Current structural complexity is primarily driven by historical decisions (LBO, acquisitions, IPO structure) rather than operational necessity.


**Supporting Evidence Sought:**
  - Entities that exist solely due to historical transactions (Inception entities from 2016 LBO)
  - Acquired companies (Datapipe, Onica, TriCore) persisting as separate legal entities without operational rationale
  - Dual public-private structure from 2020 IPO creating governance complexity beyond peer companies
  - Debt structure traceable to 2016 LBO maintained through refinancings
  - Management statements acknowledging historical complexity as burden

**Disconfirming Evidence Sought:**
  - Evidence that Inception entities serve current operational purpose (not just tax)
  - Evidence that acquired companies must remain separate due to regulatory/customer requirements
  - Evidence that public-private hybrid structure provides strategic advantage
  - Evidence that complexity was deliberately designed for current strategy
  - Peers with similar complexity for operational (not historical) reasons
**Status:** ✅ SUPPORTED  

**Notes:** SUPPORTING EVIDENCE FOUND: (1) Inception Parent and Inception Intermediate persist with no disclosed operational purpose - exist solely because they were 2016 acquisition vehicles. (2) Acquired entities (Datapipe, Onica) listed in subsidiary records but no evidence of continued separate operations justifying separate legal entities. (3) Hybrid public-private structure (Apollo 53-57% + public listing) is UNUSUAL - typical PE exit is either full sale or complete IPO with sponsor exiting to <10%. Structure exists because Apollo chose partial exit in 2020, not operational necessity. (4) Debt quantum ($1.3B) and leverage ratio trace to 2016 LBO - has been refinanced but not eliminated, showing PATH DEPENDENCY from initial leverage decision. (5) No management statements found celebrating complexity as advantage - only cost-cutting and simplification efforts (layoffs, restructuring). DISCONFIRMING EVIDENCE SOUGHT BUT NOT FOUND: No evidence that complexity is operationally necessary. No evidence of regulatory requirements forcing entity separation. No evidence of customer contracts requiring separate entities. CONCLUSION: Complexity is HISTORICAL ARTIFACT, not operational design. Current structure is product of past decisions that cannot be easily unwound, not current strategic intent. Hypothesis STRONGLY SUPPORTED.

**Irreversibility Driver:** Company valuation has collapsed from $4.3B (2016 LBO) to ~$120M market cap (2026) plus $1.3B debt = $1.42B enterprise value. Equity cannot be raised to delever - stock at $0.48 means dilution would be massive. **Why It Persists:** Restrictive covenants are IRREVERSIBLE until debt is repaid or refinanced - but company cannot refinance because: (1) Credit quality has deteriorated (stock at $0.48 signals equity worthless, approaching debt impairment), (2) No equity financing available at current valuation, (3) Asset sales restricted by covenants and would trigger lender consent requirements, (4) EBITDA insufficient to support additional debt.
