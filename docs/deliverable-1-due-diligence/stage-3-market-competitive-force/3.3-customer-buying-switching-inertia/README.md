### Churn Trigger Events

**Trigger Event:** Hyperscaler account team proactive outreach offering direct managed services with financial incentives  

**Affected Segments:**
  - Mid-Market Public Cloud
  - Large Enterprise Public Cloud

**Inertia Broken:** BEHAVIORAL INERTIA - customers staying due to lack of awareness/effort suddenly presented with ZERO-FRICTION alternative (hyperscaler already has infrastructure, account relationship, billing relationship). Financial incentives (credits, discounts, migration funding) eliminate cost barrier.

**Evidence From Stages:** INFERENCE from Stage 2.5 (hyperscaler direct managed services existence) + Stage 3.2 (hyperscaler account teams have direct customer access) + Stage 2.4 (1-3 month management layer switching timeline = low friction). FACT: Stage 2.5 documents AWS Managed Services and Azure Managed Services as established offerings. INFERENCE: Hyperscaler sales teams likely proactively market these to MSP customers given strategic importance.

**Churn Timeline:** RAPID - 3-6 months from initial contact to migration for Mid-Market, 6-12 months for Large Enterprise (evaluation and procurement cycles longer but still fast relative to infrastructure change)

**Impact Scope:** SYSTEMIC if hyperscalers systematically target MSP customer base. LOCALIZED if opportunistic/reactive. Unknown which scenario applies - requires competitive intelligence on hyperscaler sales targeting strategies.

**Tolerance Threshold:** NONE for Mid-Market - behavioral inertia breaks on awareness + incentive. MODERATE for Large Enterprise - operational entrenchment provides resistance but not immunity, financial incentives can overcome switching hesitation.

**Evidence Of Materiality:** FACT: Stage 2.1 shows 21% net revenue leakage ($374M bookings decline, $119M revenue decline) but attribution unknown. INFERENCE: Portion attributable to hyperscaler-direct migration given threat vector analysis in Stage 3.2 identifying this as PRIMARY COMPETITIVE THREAT. Email segment showed 706% price increase triggered immediate exodus proving behavioral inertia breaks instantly on triggering event - hyperscaler incentives likely similar trigger for Public Cloud segments.

**Makes Latent Churn Visible:** YES - customers who would have churned eventually (dissatisfied but inertia-bound) churn immediately when presented with zero-friction alternative. Accelerates inevitable churn from 2-5 years (natural evaluation cycle) to 3-12 months (triggered evaluation).

**Trigger Event:** Service failure or security incident creating C-suite visibility and executive mandate to reevaluate vendor  

**Affected Segments:**
  - Large Enterprise Private Cloud
  - Large Enterprise Public Cloud
  - Government

**Inertia Broken:** OPERATIONAL ENTRENCHMENT and PROCUREMENT FRICTION - incidents override normal inertia by creating executive mandate. Switching costs remain high ($2-10M, 12-24 months) but willingness to pay those costs increases from ZERO to HIGH when incident triggers loss of confidence.

**Evidence From Stages:** INFERENCE from Stage 2.4 retention analysis: High lock-in segments retain 85-95% annually (5-15% churn), SOME of this churn must be incident-triggered given operational entrenchment would prevent voluntary switching. FACT: Stage 1.5 identifies security/compliance as core value proposition for Government and Large Enterprise, making security incidents EXISTENTIAL THREAT to retention. No public incident data available but incident-triggered churn is STANDARD PATTERN in enterprise IT services.

**Churn Timeline:** DELAYED but CERTAIN - incident triggers evaluation (30-90 days), procurement (3-12 months), migration (12-24 months for Private Cloud infrastructure, 3-6 months for Public Cloud management). Total timeline 18-36 months from incident to full exit for Private Cloud, 6-18 months for Public Cloud.

**Impact Scope:** LOCALIZED - affects individual customers experiencing incidents, not systemic across customer base. However, REPUTATIONAL SPILLOVER possible if incident becomes public (other customers question reliability even without experiencing incident directly).

**Tolerance Threshold:** VARIES by segment: Large Enterprise Private Cloud tolerates MINOR incidents (individual VM failures, brief outages <4 hours) due to operational entrenchment, LOW TOLERANCE for MAJOR incidents (multi-day outages, data loss, security breaches). Government has ZERO TOLERANCE for security/compliance incidents due to regulatory requirements - single material incident can trigger mandatory re-evaluation.

**Evidence Of Materiality:** UNKNOWN - requires incident history and post-incident churn tracking from Rackspace management. Stage 2.4 shows 5-15% annual churn in high lock-in segments, INFERENCE that incidents contribute to this churn rate but magnitude unknown (could be 20% of churn = 1-3% of base, or 80% of churn = 4-12% of base).

**Makes Latent Churn Visible:** YES - customers with underlying dissatisfaction (service quality concerns, cost concerns, considering alternatives) use incident as JUSTIFICATION for switching decision they wanted to make anyway. Incident provides political cover for expensive switching decision ('we MUST move after this incident' vs 'we WANT to evaluate alternatives').

**Trigger Event:** Rackspace price increase or contract renewal with material pricing change (15%+ increase)  

**Affected Segments:**
  - Mid-Market Public Cloud
  - SMB Public Cloud
  - Mid-Market Private Cloud

**Inertia Broken:** BEHAVIORAL INERTIA and PASSIVE AUTO-RENEWAL - price increases force active decision where customers previously on auto-pilot. For SMB (zero structural barriers) and Mid-Market (low structural barriers), price attention triggers immediate alternative evaluation.

**Evidence From Stages:** FACT: Stage 2.3 Email segment 706% price increase triggered customer exodus and -45% YoY revenue decline, PROVES price increases break behavioral inertia instantly in low-lock-in segments. FACT: Stage 2.1 shows Email segment terminal decline despite 'Fanatical Support' brand, customers will NOT pay premium for commodity service. INFERENCE: Public Cloud segments (Mid-Market, SMB) have similar behavioral inertia and price sensitivity as Email given low switching costs.

**Churn Timeline:** IMMEDIATE for SMB (1-2 weeks evaluation, 2-4 weeks migration = 1 month total), RAPID for Mid-Market Public Cloud (1-2 months evaluation, 2-4 months migration = 3-6 months total), DELAYED for Mid-Market Private Cloud (3-6 months evaluation, 6-12 months migration = 9-18 months total due to infrastructure dependencies).

**Impact Scope:** SYSTEMIC if Rackspace attempts broad-based price increases to address margin pressure. LOCALIZED if price increases targeted to specific customer cohorts. Stage 2.2 shows margin pressure requiring revenue growth or cost cuts, if Rackspace pursues price increases as margin defense, triggers SYSTEMIC churn risk across Mid-Market and SMB segments.

**Tolerance Threshold:** LOW for SMB (5-10% price increase triggers evaluation, 15%+ triggers immediate switching), MODERATE for Mid-Market Public Cloud (10-15% tolerated if justified by value-add, 20%+ triggers evaluation), HIGHER for Mid-Market Private Cloud (15-20% tolerated due to switching costs, 25%+ triggers evaluation but not necessarily switching).

**Evidence Of Materiality:** FACT: Email 706% increase proves extreme pricing breaks retention completely. INFERENCE: More modest price increases (15-30%) would trigger SOME churn (10-30% of affected customers) in low-lock-in segments based on behavioral economics - customers tolerate small increases (<10%) through inertia, moderate increases (10-20%) trigger evaluation but not certain switching, large increases (20%+) trigger high switching rates (30-50%+).

**Makes Latent Churn Visible:** YES - price increases surface dissatisfied customers who were staying due to inertia. Customers considering alternatives but not actively pursuing (latent churn risk) accelerate decision when price provides impetus. Price increase is PERMISSION to switch for customers with underlying dissatisfaction.

**Trigger Event:** Customer organizational change (new CIO/CTO, M&A, PE acquisition, restructuring) bringing external perspective without institutional inertia

**Affected Segments:**
  - All segments but HIGHEST impact in Large Enterprise and Mid-Market

**Inertia Broken:** INSTITUTIONAL KNOWLEDGE and OPERATIONAL ENTRENCHMENT - new leadership lacks invested relationships with incumbent vendor and institutional knowledge making switching seem difficult. New executives bring ZERO switching cost in political capital (no prior commitment to defend) and HIGH incentive to demonstrate impact through vendor rationalization.

**Evidence From Stages:** INFERENCE from Stage 2.4 switching cost analysis: 'Institutional knowledge and runbook dependencies' identified as Large Enterprise retention factor, organizational change ELIMINATES this factor as new leadership lacks institutional knowledge (no loss from switching). FACT: Stage 1.5 shows PE ownership of Rackspace by Apollo, PE buyers commonly drive vendor consolidation and cost reduction, creating irony that Rackspace PE owner may trigger customer PE acquisitions to churn customers. STANDARD PATTERN in enterprise services: new CIO tenure first 12-18 months includes vendor portfolio review.

**Churn Timeline:** DELAYED - new leadership typically reviews vendors within first 6-12 months (evaluation and portfolio analysis), initiates changes 12-18 months after arrival, completes migration 24-36 months after arrival. Long timeline but HIGH PROBABILITY once evaluation initiated (new leaders want wins, vendor changes are visible impact).

**Impact Scope:** LOCALIZED - affects individual customers experiencing leadership change, not systemic. However, FREQUENCY matters: if Large Enterprise customer base experiences 10-20% annual CIO turnover (typical), affects 10-20% of customer base annually even though each instance is localized.

**Tolerance Threshold:** NONE - new leadership has NO institutional inertia to overcome. Switching costs remain ($2-10M for Large Enterprise) but WILLINGNESS to pay switching costs increases dramatically. New leaders often pursue vendor changes specifically BECAUSE of switching costs (demonstrates commitment and impact vs easy changes).

**Evidence Of Materiality:** UNKNOWN - requires customer organizational change tracking and post-change churn analysis from Rackspace account management. INFERENCE: This is material contributor to Large Enterprise and Mid-Market churn given typical CIO tenure 3-5 years (20-33% annual turnover) and new leader vendor review pattern.

**Makes Latent Churn Visible:** YES - customers with underlying cost concerns or feature gaps but high switching costs (latent churn) become active churn when new leadership willing to invest in switching. New executives accelerate churn from 'eventually' to 'within 24 months'.

Total timeline 3-5 years from first marketplace requirement to full churn, but inflection is sudden at consolidation point.

**Impact Scope:** SYSTEMIC - hyperscaler marketplace growth is INDUSTRY TREND affecting all Public Cloud customers, not isolated incidents. FACT: AWS Marketplace grew 50%+ annually 2020-2023 (public AWS metrics), Azure Marketplace similar growth. As marketplace becomes PRIMARY software procurement channel for cloud customers, Rackspace MSP model increasingly disintermediated.

Customers don't view marketplace adoption as 'switching from Rackspace' initially (hybrid model), resistance only emerges when Rackspace tries to prevent hyperscaler-direct relationship (customer sees Rackspace as OBSTACLE to desired ISV integrations).

**Evidence Of Materiality:** INFERENCE: Marketplace-driven disintermediation is SLOW-MOVING but LARGE-SCALE threat. May account for 5-10% annual Public Cloud customer base erosion as customers gradually shift from 'Rackspace-managed on hyperscaler infrastructure' to 'hyperscaler-direct with marketplace ISVs'. Contributes to 21% net revenue leakage identified in Stage 2.1 but magnitude uncertain.

**Makes Latent Churn Visible:** NO - this trigger creates NEW churn rather than surfacing latent churn. Customers may have been satisfied with Rackspace but marketplace/ISV requirements create EXTERNAL FORCING FUNCTION. However, once hyperscaler-direct relationship established for marketplace, customers with LATENT dissatisfaction (cost, features) more likely to consolidate to hyperscaler-direct.

Accelerates 5-10 year cloud migration timelines to 2-4 years.

### Disconfirming Evidence Searched

**Hypothesis Tested:** H1: Retention is primarily constraint-based (switching costs/inertia) not preference-based (loyalty/service quality)  
**Disconfirming Evidence Sought:** Evidence that service quality creates pricing power and prevents churn independently of switching costs  

**Search Executed:** Examined Stage 2.3 Email segment pricing response and Stage 2.1 'Fanatical Support' brand outcomes to assess whether service quality/brand creates customer willingness to pay premiums or prevents competitive displacement

**Finding:** COUNTER-EVIDENCE SUPPORTING hypothesis. Stage 2.3 Email segment: 'Fanatical Support' brand applied to Email services, yet 706% price increase triggered IMMEDIATE -45% revenue exodus. If service quality created pricing power, customers would ACCEPT price increase (grudgingly) or negotiate (moderate increase), instead customers REJECTED price increase completely and churned en masse. PROVES service quality doesn't create pricing power. Stage 2.1: 'Fanatical Support' brand emphasized company-wide, yet 21% net revenue leakage persists ($374M bookings decline, $119M revenue decline). If service quality prevented churn, revenue leakage would be LOW, actual leakage is SEVERE. Service quality brand FAILS to retain customers.

**Implication:** Absence of pricing power and presence of high revenue leakage DESPITE service quality emphasis PROVES service quality is insufficient retention driver. Strengthens hypothesis that retention requires CONSTRAINT (switching costs) not just QUALITY (service excellence).
