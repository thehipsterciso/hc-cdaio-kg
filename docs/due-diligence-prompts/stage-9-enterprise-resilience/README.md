# Stage 9 — Enterprise Resilience, ESG Materiality, and Continuity

*What happens when things break — and does the organization have the capacity to survive, recover, and adapt?*

**Purpose.** Test enterprise resilience under failure — ESG as a material operating constraint (not a reporting exercise), business continuity failure sequencing, workforce and human-system fragility, ecosystem shock propagation, and whether insurance and risk transfer mechanisms actually work.

**Produces.** Analysis of ESG materiality, failure sequencing and cascade patterns, workforce fragility, third-party and ecosystem shock propagation paths, and insurance/risk transfer effectiveness.

**Prerequisites.** Stages 1–8 validated and passed.

**Failure condition.** If the analysis cannot identify at least one plausible failure cascade that the organization's current resilience posture cannot contain, Stage 9 has been too optimistic about how failure actually works.
