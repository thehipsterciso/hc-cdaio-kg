STAGE 9.3 — WORKFORCE, TALENT, AND HUMAN-SYSTEM FRAGILITY
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Human Capital Fragility Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 9.3 ONLY.

YOU MUST NOT:
- Perform workforce optimization or org design
- Assess “talent strategy” or future hiring plans
- Use headcount totals as a proxy for resilience
- Normalize attrition or burnout as “industry standard”
- Propose remediation, incentives, or restructuring

MISSION (NON-NEGOTIABLE):
Determine whether the enterprise’s ability to operate under stress
depends on specific people, informal knowledge, brittle labor models,
or human heroics that cannot be sustained.

This is not talent management.
This is HUMAN DEPENDENCY RISK.

FAIL-FAST CONDITION:
If you cannot name at least ONE specific function or capability
whose failure depends on people rather than systems,
this sub-stage FAILS.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where key-person or key-team dependencies exist
- Where tribal knowledge substitutes for documentation
- Which roles are impossible to backfill quickly
- Where attrition or burnout becomes an operational risk
- How human fragility interacts with technical and continuity risks

Outputs must surface HUMAN SINGLE POINTS OF FAILURE.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

TASK 1 — IDENTIFY HUMAN-CRITICAL FUNCTIONS
Identify functions or capabilities ONLY if:
- Loss of people halts revenue, service delivery, or compliance
- Knowledge is not fully documented or systematized
- Substitution requires >90 days or specialized clearance
- External vendors cannot realistically replace the function

For each function, you MUST:
- Name the function or capability precisely
- Describe what fails if the people are unavailable
- Identify whether dependency is INDIVIDUAL, TEAM, or NETWORK

TASK 2 — MAP DEPENDENCY TYPES
For each identified function, classify dependency as:
- KEY_PERSON (single individual)
- KEY_TEAM (small, tightly coupled group)
- TRIBAL_KNOWLEDGE (undocumented, experience-based)
- VENDOR_EMBEDDED (people embedded via third parties)

You MUST explain WHY dependency persists.

TASK 3 — STRESS ATTRITION AND ABSENCE
Explicitly test:
- What happens if this person/team is unavailable for 30 / 90 / 180 days
- Whether knowledge transfer is feasible under stress
- Whether replacement requires trust, credentials, or customer approval
- Whether workload concentration causes burnout amplification

TASK 4 — ORGANIZATIONAL BEHAVIOR UNDER PRESSURE
You MUST surface:
- Where heroics are expected or normalized
- Where burnout signals are visible but tolerated
- Where management assumes “people will step up”
- Where fragility is hidden by short-term performance

TASK 5 — TOUCH TEST (MANDATORY)
For each human dependency:
- State what breaks first when people are lost
- Identify secondary operational or compliance failures
- Classify severity: LOW | MED | HIGH (justify by consequence)
- State reversibility vs irreversibility

TASK 6 — HYPOTHESIS DISCIPLINE (STAGE 0.3 — MANDATORY)
You MUST define explicit, falsifiable hypotheses (minimum 3), such as:
- H1: “Critical operations rely on undocumented human knowledge.”
- H2: “Key-person dependencies exist despite formal processes.”
- H3: “Attrition risk compounds during stress events.”

For each hypothesis:
- Define SUPPORTING evidence sought
- Define DISCONFIRMING evidence sought
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update status: SUPPORTED | WEAKENED | REFUTED | INCONCLUSIVE
- Record evidence not found

TASK 7 — UNCERTAINTY BOUNDING (STAGE 0.4 — MANDATORY)
You MUST:
- Record UNKNOWN vs UNKNOWABLE human risks
- State decision impact if ignored
- State what would reduce uncertainty
- State risk of false confidence
