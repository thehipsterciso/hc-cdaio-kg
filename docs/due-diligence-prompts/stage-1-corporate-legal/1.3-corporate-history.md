STAGE 1.3 — CORPORATE HISTORY AS STRUCTURAL DEBT
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Structural History & Path Dependency Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 1.3 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Provide a corporate history narrative or timeline for its own sake
- Repeat legal ownership analysis from Stage 1.1
- Repeat control analysis from Stage 1.2
- Perform operational coupling analysis (that occurs in Stage 1.4)
- Assess financial performance or strategy implications
- Propose remediation, simplification, or optimization

History matters ONLY insofar as it constrains present and future options.

If history is summarized without identifying present-day constraints,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which past decisions now function as irreversible or high-friction constraints
- Which structures exist solely because of historical events
- Where optionality was permanently reduced
- Where “temporary” arrangements hardened into structure
- Why current complexity exists even if it is no longer rational

Outputs must surface HISTORICAL FACTS THAT NOW OVERRIDE CURRENT INTENT.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material historical events ONLY if they:
- Created or materially altered legal entities, platforms, or structures
- Introduced fragmentation, duplication, or stranded assets
- Required partial or failed integration
- Produced long-lived exceptions, carve-outs, or special handling
- Still shape control, structure, or operability today

Events may include:
- Acquisitions or mergers
- Divestitures or spin-offs
- Major reorganizations or restructurings
- Platform migrations or abandoned transformations
- Regulatory settlements with structural consequences

2. Map historical artifacts to present constraints:
For each event, identify:
- The structural artifact created (entity, system, contract, process)
- The current constraint that artifact imposes
- Whether the constraint is legal, operational, contractual, or combined

3. Identify path dependency and irreversibility:
Explicitly surface:
- Structures that cannot be unwound without severe disruption
- Decisions that permanently reduced future optionality
- Integration work that stopped halfway — and why
- “Temporary” solutions that became permanent by default

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each historical artifact:
- State what would break if it were removed, reversed, or altered today
- Classify breakage as LEGAL, OPERATIONAL, CONTROL, or COMBINED
- Identify whether breakage is immediate, cascading, or latent

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Historical decisions as primary drivers of current complexity
- Accumulation of structural debt through repeated partial changes
- Inability to unwind prior platform or structural bets

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- M&A announcements and regulatory filings
- Reorganization or restructuring disclosures
- Platform migration or transformation announcements
- Historical segment reporting changes
- Post-merger integration disclosures (if any)
- Management commentary explicitly referencing legacy decisions

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Explicitly call out gaps, silence, or missing disclosures
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT narratively resolve gaps
