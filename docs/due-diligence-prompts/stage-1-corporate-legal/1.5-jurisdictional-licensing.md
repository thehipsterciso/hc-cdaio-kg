STAGE 1.5 — JURISDICTIONAL, LICENSING, AND STRUCTURAL COMPLIANCE LOCKS
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Jurisdictional Lock-In Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 1.5 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Perform general regulatory compliance analysis (that occurs in later stages)
- Assess compliance maturity or effectiveness
- Propose remediation, relocation, or restructuring
- Treat jurisdictional flexibility as an assumption
- Describe global operations without identifying immobility

Your task is to determine where the enterprise is STRUCTURALLY IMMOVABLE
due to jurisdiction, license, legal form, or sovereign constraint.

This is NOT regulatory compliance.
This is STRUCTURAL IMMOBILITY.

If the enterprise is described as “globally flexible” without refutation,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which activities or assets are legally bound to specific jurisdictions
- Which licenses are entity-, geography-, or nationality-specific
- Where public-sector, government, or sovereign customers impose constraints
- Where national security or sector-specific laws override corporate intent
- Where consolidation, relocation, or integration is legally impossible or highly constrained

Outputs must surface JURISDICTIONAL AND LICENSING FACTS THAT OVERRIDE INTENT.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify structural lock-ins ONLY if they:
- Tie licensed activities to specific legal entities or jurisdictions
- Bind operations to local presence, nationality, or residency requirements
- Arise from public-sector or government customer obligations
- Trigger national security, sovereignty, or sector-specific constraints
- Prevent transfer, consolidation, or relocation without external approval

2. Map lock-ins to assets and operations:
For each lock-in, identify:
- The activity, asset, or capability involved
- The entity and/or jurisdiction to which it is locked
- The legal or regulatory basis for immobility
- The operational consequence of attempting to move or consolidate

3. Identify non-transferability and immobility:
Explicitly surface:
- Licenses that cannot be transferred or assigned
- Operations that must remain in-country or in-entity
- Workforce, data, or infrastructure subject to local mandates
- Situations where change would void authorization or eligibility

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each lock-in:
- State what breaks if the activity, asset, or entity is moved or consolidated
- Classify impact as LEGAL, OPERATIONAL, CONTRACTUAL, or COMBINED
- Identify whether impact is immediate, conditional, or absolute

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Assumed global flexibility vs actual jurisdictional immobility
- Licensing as a primary constraint on integration
- Sovereign or national security requirements as veto points

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Licensing and authorization disclosures
- Public-sector and government contract disclosures
- Regulatory filings tied to operational authorization
- National security, sovereignty, or sector-specific laws
- Government customer eligibility or residency requirements

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat statutes, licenses, and government requirements as primary evidence
- Explicitly identify undisclosed or opaque licensing regimes
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
