# Stage Report Prompt

Stage 1 Report Definition

Corporate, Legal, and Structural Reality

What This Chapter 
Is
 as a Deliverable

Stage 1 is the “physics layer” of the enterprise.
It establishes the non-negotiable structural facts that determine what any strategy, transaction, integration, or operating model can and cannot do.

This chapter answers one question relentlessly:

“What breaks, blocks, or becomes illegal if we try to change this company?”

It is not descriptive.
It is constraint-revealing.

How This Chapter Is Used (Practically)

This chapter is consumed by:

Deal leads (Corp Dev / PE / Strategy)
Integration architects
Legal + tax leads
Security, data, and platform leaders
Board members evaluating feasibility, not vision

It is read before:

Synergy models
Integration roadmaps
Platform consolidation plans
Data, security, or operating model redesigns

If Stage 1 is wrong or incomplete, every downstream stage is fiction.

What the Reader Walks Away Knowing

After reading Stage 1, a competent operator should be able to say:

“These entities cannot be touched.”
“These contracts will detonate under change-of-control.”
“This data cannot be centralized without renegotiation.”
“This license anchors us to a jurisdiction.”
“This ‘simple integration’ is structurally impossible.”

If they still believe the company is flexible by default, the chapter failed.

Narrative Shape of the Chapter (Important)

Although the work is analytical, the report itself is not a data dump.

The chapter reads as:

1. Executive Structural Truth Summary (2–3 pages)

A blunt, non-technical synthesis:

Top structural constraints
Major control asymmetries
Deal-breaking or deal-shaping facts
Explicit invalidation of common assumptions

This section alone should make a board uncomfortable — productively.

2. Structural Reality Maps (Core Body)

Each sub-stage contributes maps, not prose:

1.1 Legal–Operational Control Map
Shows:

Which entities matter
What they actually control
What they block
Why they exist

This replaces org charts and legal trees.

1.2 Control & Economic Reality Map
Shows:

Who can stop change
Who benefits from change
Who controls outcomes without appearing to

This is where “ownership ≠ control” becomes undeniable.

1.3 Structural Debt & Path Dependency Map
Shows:

Which past decisions hardened into constraints
Which “temporary” structures became permanent
Why integration stopped where it did

This reframes history as present-day liability.

1.4 Contractual Friction Map
Shows:

What breaks under transaction or integration
Where leverage shifts post-change
Where data, customers, or vendors gain veto power

Contracts are treated as governance mechanisms, not legal paperwork.

1.5 Jurisdictional & Licensing Lock-In Map
Shows:

Where mobility is illusion
Which assets cannot move
Which entities cannot merge
Which operations are jurisdiction-anchored

This destroys “we can always restructure later” thinking.

3. Explicit False Assumptions Invalidated (Mandatory Section)

This section matters more than most diligence teams realize.

Examples:

“The company can consolidate data centrally” → False
“The platform is globally deployable” → False
“We can simplify legal structure post-close” → False

Each assumption is:

Stated explicitly
Refuted with evidence
Tied to a concrete constraint

What This Chapter Deliberately Avoids

This chapter does not:

Propose solutions
Suggest integration plans
Optimize structure
Recommend legal actions

Those come later.

Stage 1 only answers:

“What is structurally true, whether we like it or not?”

Quality Bar (How You Know It’s Good)

This chapter is successful if:

Strategy teams feel constrained but informed
Legal teams feel accurately understood
Integration teams stop making naive assumptions
Someone says:￼“I didn’t realize that was non-optional.”

If no one changes their mental model of the enterprise, it failed.

One-Sentence Definition (If You Need It)

Stage 1 is a legal-operational truth map that defines the immutable constraints governing ownership, control, data, people, and change — before strategy is allowed to exist.
