STAGE 8.1 — CYBERSECURITY REALITY & CONTROL FAILURE SURFACE
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Breach Reality & Control Power Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 8.1 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Perform security maturity or framework assessments
- Inventory controls, tools, or policies
- Recommend remediation or improvements
- Normalize incidents as “contained” or “resolved”
- Assume controls operate as documented

Your task is to determine HOW THE ENTERPRISE ACTUALLY FAILS
under cyber stress — and how that failure constrains business behavior.

If you cannot name systems, domains, or activities that are effectively
“too risky to touch,” this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where security controls actually block action vs document intent
- Where incidents exposed fragility or near-existential risk
- Which systems are frozen due to accumulated risk
- Who gains de facto veto power by invoking security risk
- Where risk is repeatedly “accepted” because change is impossible

Outputs must surface SECURITY FACTS THAT OVERRIDE INTENT.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material control failure surfaces ONLY if they:
- Have experienced disclosed incidents or near-misses
- Are explicitly scoped out of certifications due to risk
- Are repeatedly referenced as “legacy,” “sensitive,” or “high risk”
- Require manual or heroic intervention to keep secure
- Are avoided or deferred during transformation efforts

2. For each failure surface, map:
- The system, platform, or domain involved
- The failure mode (technical, organizational, or control-based)
- The business impact if exploited
- Whether the risk is mitigated, tolerated, or frozen
- Who can block change by citing this risk

3. Examine incident truth vs narrative:
For disclosed incidents:
- Capture the public description
- Infer likely actual impact based on evidence
- Identify what was minimized, reframed, or omitted
- Explain why the gap matters operationally

4. Identify non-negotiable security constraints:
Explicitly surface:
- Systems that cannot be modernized without reintroducing risk
- Environments where compliance scope prevents change
- Activities prohibited due to unacceptable blast radius
- Repeatedly accepted risks that signal immobility

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Security risk as a de facto veto mechanism
- Control failures as drivers of technical immobility
- Incidents shaping long-term operating constraints

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Public incident disclosures (SEC, press, regulators)
- Customer advisories and postmortems
- Certification scope statements and exclusions
- Security leadership turnover or hiring patterns
- Insurance disclosures (cyber coverage limits/exclusions)

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Treat incident disclosures as primary evidence
- Explicitly call out silence or absence of disclosure
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth gaps
