STAGE 8 — ORIENTATION
––––––––––––––––––––

STAGE PURPOSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Stage 8 exists to identify HARD, ENFORCEABLE, and IRREVERSIBLE
boundaries on the enterprise.

This stage answers the question:

“What is the company legally, operationally, or technically
FORBIDDEN to do — and who can enforce that prohibition?”

It determines where ambition, strategy, or intent
is overridden by risk, law, security, privacy, or power.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 8 IS:

- A discovery of non-negotiable constraints
- A mapping of risk as a blocking force, not a management exercise
- An identification of who can halt change outright
- A test of where violations would create existential damage
- A separation of theoretical freedom from enforceable reality

Stage 8 focuses on:

- Cybersecurity failure surfaces and frozen systems
- Privacy, consent, and data use prohibitions
- Regulatory enforcement and intervention power
- Data residency, sovereignty, and geographic immutability
- Governance veto rights and escalation chokepoints

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS NOT
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 8 is NOT:

- A security maturity assessment
- A control checklist or framework mapping
- A policy or compliance inventory
- A risk register or heat map
- A “how to fix it” exercise

If this stage could be mistaken for a compliance review,
it has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
STRUCTURE OF STAGE 8 (NO EXECUTION)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 8 will be executed through the following atomic sub-stages:

8.1 Cybersecurity Reality & Control Failure Surface  
→ Where systems are effectively frozen due to risk and failure history

8.2 Privacy, Data Rights, and Use-Limitation Reality  
→ What data cannot legally or contractually be used, combined, or monetized

8.3 Regulatory Enforcement, Surveillance, and Intervention Risk  
→ Who can materially constrain or halt operations fastest

8.4 Data Residency, Sovereignty, and Geographic Immutability  
→ Where data and processing are physically and legally stuck

8.5 Governance Power, Veto Rights, and Risk Escalation Reality  
→ Who can stop change without formally saying “no”

Each sub-stage:
- Is constraint-driven, not maturity-driven
- Must invalidate at least one assumed freedom
- Must identify enforceable power or immobility
- Must preserve uncertainty and bound unknowns
- Will emit evidence-backed, atomic JSON outputs

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 8 FAILURE CONDITIONS (GLOBAL)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 8 has FAILED if:

- Risk is described without identifying what is blocked
- Security is framed as posture rather than failure modes
- Privacy is discussed without invalidating data use cases
- Regulation is treated as theoretical rather than enforceable
- Governance is described without naming veto chokepoints
- Constraints are framed as “manageable” without proof

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 8 EXIT CRITERIA (PRE-DECLARED)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 8 will FAIL unless, upon validation:

1. At least 5 hard constraints are identified and evidenced  
2. At least 2 commonly assumed freedoms are explicitly invalidated  
3. At least 1 power center capable of blocking enterprise change is named  
4. At least 1 data or workload immutability is demonstrated  
5. Unknowns are explicitly bounded with business consequences
