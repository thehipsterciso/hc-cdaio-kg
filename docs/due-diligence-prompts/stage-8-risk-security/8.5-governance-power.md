STAGE 8.5 — GOVERNANCE POWER, VETO RIGHTS, AND RISK ESCALATION REALITY
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Organizational Power & Veto Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 8.5 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Perform board composition or independence analysis
- Summarize governance structures at a descriptive level
- Assume formal authority equals real power
- Treat governance as neutral or procedural
- Propose governance reforms or escalation paths

Your task is to determine WHO CAN BLOCK CHANGE WITHOUT SAYING “NO”,
and HOW THAT BLOCKAGE IS ENFORCED IN PRACTICE.

If you cannot identify at least one real veto chokepoint,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where risk escalation is used to halt initiatives
- Who controls approval forums and agendas
- Where governance is performative vs binding
- Which roles or bodies act as structural chokepoints
- How ambiguity, delay, or “safety” is weaponized

Outputs must surface GOVERNANCE POWER THAT OVERRIDES INTENT.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify veto power centers ONLY if they:
- Can delay or block initiatives through risk framing
- Control funding release, approval gates, or sign-offs
- Influence regulators, auditors, or insurers
- Are repeatedly cited as reasons initiatives stall or die
- Operate without clear accountability for blockage

2. For each veto power center, identify:
- The role, committee, or body
- Formal authority (if any)
- Informal or de facto power exercised
- Mechanism by which change is blocked
- Typical escalation path and outcome

3. Identify governance chokepoints:
Explicitly surface:
- Approval forums where initiatives stall
- Risk committees that override business decisions
- Roles that invoke ambiguity to defer action
- Areas where accountability diffuses into process

4. Examine escalation asymmetry:
Explicitly test:
- Who can escalate risk and be believed
- Who escalates but is ignored
- Where escalation favors inaction over action
- Where “not safe enough” consistently wins

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Risk governance functioning as a veto mechanism
- Informal power outweighing formal authority
- Escalation dynamics favoring delay or stasis

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Board and committee scope disclosures
- Risk committee charters and descriptions
- Escalation language in filings or policies
- Historical initiative failures (inferred from disclosures)
- Leadership tenure, churn, or role overlap patterns

You MUST:
- Label claims as FACT or INFERENCE
- Treat disclosed governance scopes as primary evidence
- Explicitly call out ambiguity or silence
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or normalize power dynamics
