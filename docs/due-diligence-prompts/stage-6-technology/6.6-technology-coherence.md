STAGE 6.6 — TECHNOLOGY ESTATE COHERENCE & CONTRADICTION
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 6.6 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Produce an architecture overview
- Propose consolidation, standardization, or modernization
- Evaluate technology “best practices”
- Normalize contradictions as trade-offs
- Describe future-state alignment

Your task is to determine whether the technology estate
is INTERNALLY COHERENT or sustained through
CONTRADICTION, EXCEPTION, AND WORKAROUND.

If the estate is labeled “coherent” without surfacing tension,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where architectural principles conflict in practice
- Where parallel systems perform overlapping roles
- Where centralization and decentralization collide
- Where platforms exist solely to compensate for others
- Where scale increases complexity instead of reducing it

Outputs must surface TECHNOLOGY CONTRADICTIONS
THAT UNDERMINE EXECUTION AND CONTROL.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material estate contradictions ONLY if they:
- Create duplicated capability or parallel stacks
- Require exceptions to stated standards or principles
- Introduce manual reconciliation between systems
- Exist solely to bridge incompatible platforms
- Persist despite known cost or fragility

Contradictions may exist between:
- Legacy vs modern platforms
- Centralized vs federated systems
- Build vs buy decisions
- Global vs regional implementations
- Platform strategy vs operational reality

2. For each contradiction, determine manifestation and risk:
You MUST identify:
- How the contradiction manifests operationally
- Which teams must absorb the complexity
- What failure modes the contradiction introduces
- What business or operational risk it creates
- Why the contradiction persists

3. Identify estate-level incoherence patterns:
Explicitly surface:
- Where exceptions dominate the norm
- Where coordination cost rises with scale
- Where complexity is shifted rather than reduced
- Where “temporary” coexistence became permanent
- Where the estate violates its own stated logic

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (MANDATORY):
For each contradiction:
- State what breaks if one side of the contradiction is removed
- Identify which dependencies fail
- Classify impact as OPERATIONAL, CONTROL, RELIABILITY, or COMBINED
- Identify whether impact is immediate, cascading, or latent

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — MANDATORY):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses such as:
- “The technology estate is broadly coherent”
- “Parallel systems are transitional, not structural”
- “Scale reduces complexity in this estate”

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly
