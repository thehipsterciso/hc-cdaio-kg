STAGE 6 — ORIENTATION
––––––––––––––––––––

STAGE PURPOSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Stage 6 exists to determine where TECHNOLOGY, COMPUTE,
and PLATFORM REALITY impose NON-NEGOTIABLE CONSTRAINTS
on the enterprise regardless of intent, capital, or leadership.

This stage answers the question:

“Where does operational control, fragility, and immobility
actually live in the technology and compute estate?”

It evaluates whether the operating and financial realities
uncovered in Stages 1–5 are technically survivable,
movable, or fundamentally trapped.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 6 IS:

- A discovery of where the business is technically trapped
- A mapping of control points embedded in compute and platforms
- An exposure of fragility, immobility, and irreversible dependency
- A test of operational survivability under technical stress
- A separation of perceived flexibility from actual exit reality

Stage 6 focuses on:

- Compute and data center control reality
- Cloud dependency, hyperscaler power, and exit fiction
- Platform criticality, coupling, and blast radius
- Vendor lock-in, licensing, and commercial constraint
- Technical debt as operational and economic drag
- Internal technology coherence and contradiction

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS NOT
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 6 is NOT:

- An architecture assessment
- A modernization or migration roadmap
- A cloud maturity review
- A tooling or application inventory
- A “current state” or “target state” diagram
- A technology strategy discussion

If this stage could be mistaken for
an architecture review or transformation assessment,
it has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
STRUCTURE OF STAGE 6 (NO EXECUTION)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 6 will be executed through the following atomic sub-stages:

6.1 Compute & Data Center Control Reality  
→ Where compute physically lives, who controls it, and what breaks if it moves

6.2 Cloud Dependency, Hyperscaler Power, and Exit Fiction  
→ Whether cloud usage represents flexibility or asymmetric dependency

6.3 Platform Criticality, Coupling, and Change Fragility  
→ Which platforms the business cannot afford to touch

6.4 Vendor Lock-In, Licensing, and Commercial Constraint  
→ Where vendors exert structural operational or economic control

6.5 Technical Debt as Economic and Operational Constraint  
→ Where accumulated debt constrains execution and outcomes

6.6 Technology Estate Coherence & Contradiction  
→ Whether the estate is coherent or sustained through exception and workaround

Each sub-stage:
- Will be hypothesis-driven
- Will require disconfirming evidence searches
- Will apply “what breaks if touched” testing
- Will preserve uncertainty
- Will emit multi-file JSON outputs to prevent volume failure

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 6 FAILURE CONDITIONS (GLOBAL)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 6 has FAILED if:

- Technology is described as flexible without exit analysis
- Compute locations are listed without immobility reasoning
- Cloud usage is equated with portability
- Platforms are treated as replaceable by default
- Vendors are framed as neutral suppliers
- Technical debt is discussed without business consequence
- Architecture narrative replaces constraint discovery

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 6 EXIT CRITERIA (PRE-DECLARED)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 6 will FAIL unless, upon validation:

1. At least 3 immovable or hard-to-exit compute dependencies are identified  
2. At least 2 cloud or platform “exit fictions” are explicitly invalidated  
3. At least 2 platforms are identified as high blast-radius systems  
4. At least 1 vendor is shown to exert structural operational or economic leverage  
5. At least 1 technical debt area is shown to constrain business outcomes
