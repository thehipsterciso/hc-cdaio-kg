STAGE 4.3 — CROSS-FUNCTIONAL DEPENDENCY, BOTTLENECKS, AND FAILURE PROPAGATION
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Dependency & Failure Propagation Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 4.3 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Treat dependencies as coordination issues
- Attribute failure to communication gaps
- Normalize handoffs as necessary friction
- Propose organizational fixes or reassignments
- Collapse systemic failure into isolated incidents

Your task is to determine WHERE EXECUTION FAILURES ORIGINATE,
HOW THEY PROPAGATE,
AND WHICH STRUCTURAL DEPENDENCIES MAKE FAILURE INEVITABLE.

If dependencies are described without identifying propagation effects,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which cross-functional dependencies are structurally binding
- Where single points of failure exist (explicit or hidden)
- Where failure is absorbed vs exposed
- Which teams or functions act as execution shock absorbers
- How localized breakdowns cascade systemically
- Where volume or stress reveals latent bottlenecks

Outputs must surface FAILURE PHYSICS,
not coordination narratives.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material cross-functional dependencies ONLY if they:
- Are required for execution of critical work
- Recur across multiple workstreams
- Involve approval, handoff, or enablement functions
- Are cited as sources of delay, risk, or friction
- Concentrate load during peak execution periods

Dependencies may include (but are not limited to):
- Central platform or enablement teams
- Shared services (security, legal, finance, IT)
- Risk, compliance, or approval gates
- Architecture or standards bodies
- Scarce expert or review functions

2. Map dependency structure and load:
For each dependency, identify:
- Upstream function(s)
- Downstream function(s)
- Nature of the dependency (approval, enablement, data, capacity)
- Whether dependency is serial or parallel
- Whether load scales with volume or complexity

3. Identify bottlenecks and masked failure:
Explicitly surface:
- Where work queues form
- Where delays accumulate invisibly
- Which teams absorb failure to protect downstream delivery
- Where heroics substitute for capacity or design
- Where failure is delayed rather than prevented

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each dependency or bottleneck:
- State what breaks if the dependency is stressed
- State what breaks if the dependency is removed
- Classify breakage as OPERATIONAL, RISK, DELIVERY, or COMBINED
- Identify whether breakage is localized or systemic

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Dependency density as a driver of execution failure
- Bottlenecks as structural, not incidental
- Failure absorption masking systemic fragility

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- References to handoffs, dependencies, or coordination in disclosures
- Incident, outage, or post-mortem reports
- Commentary on shared services or centralized teams
- Repeated references to “alignment” or “cross-functional”
- Execution delays attributed to approvals or enablement
- Restructuring rationales tied to bottlenecks

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat propagation inference explicitly as inference
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or narratively resolve gaps
