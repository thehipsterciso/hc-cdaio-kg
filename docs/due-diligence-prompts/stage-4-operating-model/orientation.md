# STAGE 4 — OPERATING MODEL, ORGANIZATIONAL REALITY, AND EXECUTION PHYSICS



Purpose:
Determine how the enterprise actually executes work, where execution reliably succeeds or fails, and which organizational mechanics override intent, strategy, or leadership will.

Standard:
M&A operational diligence + execution forensics
(structure- and system-driven, not culture- or personality-driven)

Output:
Execution constraint and failure-mode map, not an operating model or transformation plan

“How does work really get done here—and where does execution predictably break regardless of intent?”

4.0 Stage Framing (MANDATORY)

What this stage IS

• An analysis of how work actually flows through the organization
• A determination of where decisions are made vs where execution occurs
• A mapping of execution latency, friction, and failure propagation
• A forensic examination of coordination, escalation, and accountability
• A test of whether the organization can reliably execute inside known constraints

What this stage IS NOT

• An org chart review
• A RACI or role-clarity exercise
• A culture, engagement, or leadership assessment
• A maturity model or capability scorecard
• An operating model, transformation, or reorg design

If this stage could be mistaken for management consulting, it has failed.

4.1 Decision Rights, Authority Flow, and Escalation Physics

Agent Role: Decision Rights & Authority Analyst

Mission (Non-Negotiable)

Determine where decisions are actually made, how authority flows in practice,
and where escalation is required to get work unstuck.

You are not mapping formal governance.
You are mapping execution reality.

Analytical Burden

You must prove:

• Where decisions are made de facto vs de jure
• Which decisions require escalation to execute
• Where authority is fragmented or duplicated
• Where decision latency structurally delays execution
• Where accountability exists without authority—or authority without accountability

Mandatory Evidence

• Governance disclosures
• Operating committee structures (where public)
• Internal escalation patterns described in filings or commentary
• Post-mortem or incident disclosures
• Repeated references to “alignment,” “coordination,” or “complexity”

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

• What decisions cannot be made locally?
• What decisions require senior escalation to execute?
• Where does escalation substitute for process?
• What breaks when escalation bandwidth is saturated?

4.2 Operating Cadence, Coordination Load, and Execution Throughput

Agent Role: Execution Throughput & Coordination Analyst

Mission

Determine whether the organization can move work through itself at scale
without coordination overload or execution collapse.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

• Core execution cadences (planning, delivery, review)
• Coordination load across teams and functions
• Bottlenecks that recur regardless of priority
• Where throughput degrades as scope or volume increases

Mandatory Evidence

• References to delivery delays or execution drag
• Dependency-heavy operating models
• Missed timelines discussed in disclosures
• Repeated restructurings tied to “speed” or “focus”
• Execution metrics (where public)

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

• What work scales poorly?
• Where coordination costs exceed value created?
• Where execution slows non-linearly?
• What must be deprioritized for anything to ship?

4.3 Cross-Functional Dependency, Bottlenecks, and Failure Propagation

Agent Role: Dependency & Failure Propagation Analyst

Mission

Identify where execution failure in one area predictably cascades elsewhere.

Analytical Burden

You must surface:

• Structural dependencies between functions
• Single points of failure masked by heroics
• Teams that absorb failure so others appear effective
• Where failure propagation is delayed but inevitable

Mandatory Evidence

• Repeated references to “handoffs” or “alignment”
• Centralized enablement or platform teams
• Incident or outage disclosures
• Persistent cross-functional friction signals

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

• Where does failure originate?
• Where is it absorbed vs exposed?
• Which teams are chronic bottlenecks?
• What fails when volume spikes?

4.4 Incentives, Accountability Dilution, and Execution Drag

Agent Role: Incentive & Accountability Analyst

Mission

Determine where incentives and accountability undermine execution reliability.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

• Misalignment between incentives and execution outcomes
• Accountability dilution across layers or functions
• Where success metrics conflict with system performance
• Where risk is displaced rather than owned

Mandatory Evidence

• Compensation disclosures
• Performance metric descriptions
• References to “shared accountability”
• Repeated missed commitments without consequence

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

• Who pays the price for failure?
• Who benefits when execution slips?
• Where accountability is symbolic?
• Where incentives reward local optimization over system health?

4.5 Talent Deployment vs System Constraints

Agent Role: Talent & System Constraint Analyst

Mission

Determine whether talent is the constraint—or whether the system neutralizes talent.

Analytical Burden

You must surface:

• Where skilled talent is unable to change outcomes
• Where execution depends on heroics
• Where turnover correlates with structural friction
• Where talent concentration masks systemic weakness

Mandatory Evidence

• Attrition or hiring commentary
• Key person risk disclosures
• Dependency on specific individuals or teams
• Repeated restructuring tied to “talent”

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

• Where would adding talent not help?
• Where does execution collapse when key people leave?
• Where does the system defeat competence?
• Where heroics are required for baseline delivery?

Stage 4 Exit Criteria (ABSOLUTE)

Stage 4 is FAILED unless:

1. At least 3 recurring execution bottlenecks are identified and evidenced
2. At least 2 decision or authority mismatches are surfaced
3. At least 1 execution failure is shown to propagate systemically
4. At least 1 place is identified where talent cannot overcome structure
5. At least 1 false belief about “execution capability” is explicitly invalidated
