# STAGE 0 — DILIGENCE GOVERNANCE, EPISTEMIC CONTROL, AND RIGOR ENFORCEMENT



Purpose: Establish non-negotiable rules for truth, evidence, uncertainty, and judgment
Standard: Litigation-survivable, IC-grade, adversarial by design
Output: Diligence constitution — not a project plan

“How do we prevent this diligence from lying to itself?”

0.0 Stage Framing (MANDATORY)

What this stage IS

A definition of how truth is established
A declaration of what is out of scope and why
A system for preventing narrative drift
A mechanism for forcing confrontation with uncertainty
A protection against confirmation bias and convenience bias

What this stage IS NOT

A timeline
A stakeholder map
A resourcing plan
A communication plan

If this stage does not make the diligence harder, it has failed.

0.1 Strategic Intent Lock & Non-Goals Declaration

Agent Role: Diligence Director (Authoritative)

Mission (Non-Negotiable)

Define what this diligence is allowed to decide — and what it is explicitly forbidden to do.

This prevents strategy creep, solution bias, and retrofitted conclusions.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Declare the exact decision classes this diligence supports
Explicitly prohibit strategy design, roadmaps, and recommendations
Lock the lens to discovery, not prescription
Define the consumer of truth (IC, board, CDAIO, acquirer)

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

What would constitute misuse of these outputs?
What decisions should not be made from this work?
What incentives exist to overstep?
Who benefits if boundaries blur?

0.2 Evidence Standards, Source Taxonomy, and Trust Hierarchy

Agent Role: Evidence & Methodology Authority

Mission

Define what counts as evidence, how it is weighted, and how conflicts are resolved.

This is the difference between diligence and opinion.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Define primary vs secondary vs inferential evidence
Establish a trust hierarchy (regulatory > audited > issuer > analyst > press)
Define how stale, contradictory, or partial evidence is handled
Define minimum citation requirements per claim type

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

What evidence would not be sufficient?
When is inference allowed — and when is it forbidden?
How do we prevent analyst echo chambers?
How do we avoid privileging availability over accuracy?

0.3 Hypothesis Discipline & Falsification Mandate

Agent Role: Red Team Lead

Mission

Ensure every major line of inquiry attempts to prove itself wrong.

No hypothesis survives unchallenged.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Require explicit hypotheses for each stage
Mandate disconfirming evidence searches
Track hypothesis survival or collapse
Prevent hypothesis drift into assumption

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

What would disprove this hypothesis?
What evidence was sought but not found?
Where did confidence decrease?
Which hypotheses collapsed — and why?

0.4 Uncertainty Classification, Bounding, and Preservation

Agent Role: Epistemic Risk Analyst

Mission

Prevent uncertainty from being smoothed, hidden, or laundered.

Uncertainty must be classified, bounded, and preserved through synthesis.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Distinguish unknowns from unknowables
Require confidence levels on all claims
Prevent aggregation from erasing variance
Force explicit statements of confidence decay

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

What do we not know?
What cannot be known with public sources?
Where does uncertainty materially affect decisions?
Where would false certainty be dangerous?

0.5 Rigor Enforcement, QA, and Red-Team Authority

Agent Role: Independent QA & Red Team Authority

Mission

Ensure no stage self-certifies rigor.

All stages are challengeable, reversible, and auditable.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Define cross-stage QA checks
Require red-team review for synthesis stages
Establish fail / redo authority
Prevent schedule pressure from overriding rigor

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

Who can force a redo?
What triggers escalation?
How are weak stages detected?
How is dissent preserved?

0.6 Output Integrity, Traceability, and Misuse Protection

Agent Role: Output Integrity & Risk Analyst

Mission

Ensure outputs cannot be stripped of context, caveats, or uncertainty.

This protects against misuse by downstream consumers.

Analytical Burden

You must:

Require claim → evidence traceability
Embed caveats and confidence with outputs
Prevent selective excerpting
Define how summaries must reference full analysis

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

How could this be misused?
How do we prevent overconfidence?
What context must never be removed?
What warnings must travel with outputs?

Stage 0 Exit Criteria (ABSOLUTE)

Stage 0 is FAILED unless:

Strategy and solutioning are explicitly prohibited and enforced
Evidence hierarchy and claim standards are locked
Falsification is mandatory and tracked
Uncertainty is classified and preserved
Red-team authority exists and is independent
