STAGE 7.7 — EPISTEMIC COHERENCE & INFORMATION SYSTEM CONTRADICTIONS
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Epistemic Integrity Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 7.7 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Reconcile contradictions narratively
- Declare a “single source of truth”
- Treat contradiction as transitional or benign
- Propose governance, tooling, or cultural remedies
- Collapse uncertainty into alignment

Your task is to determine WHETHER THE ENTERPRISE
REASONS COHERENTLY — OR FUNCTIONS THROUGH CONTRADICTION.

If coherence is asserted without exposing tension,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where contradictory data is knowingly accepted
- Where multiple “truths” coexist operationally
- Where narrative overrides measurement
- Where simplification erases material risk
- Whether epistemic coherence exists in fact or only in rhetoric

Outputs must surface EPISTEMIC FAULT LINES
that distort executive understanding.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify epistemic contradictions ONLY if they:
- Appear in executive, board, or regulatory contexts
- Involve materially different metrics or interpretations
- Persist across reporting periods
- Are explicitly acknowledged or quietly tolerated
- Influence capital, risk, or strategic decisions

2. For each contradiction, map its structure:
You MUST identify:
- The conflicting truths or metrics
- Where each appears (context, audience, forum)
- Who benefits from each version
- Why the contradiction persists
- What risk it creates

3. Identify contradiction management patterns:
Explicitly surface:
- Situations where leaders knowingly accept inconsistency
- Areas where narrative substitutes for evidence
- Domains where uncertainty is systematically hidden
- Contexts where confidence exceeds data integrity

4. Apply the COHERENCE STRESS TEST (MANDATORY):
For each contradiction:
- State what breaks if forced into reconciliation
- Identify whose authority would be challenged
- Identify decisions that rely on ambiguity
- State what risk is masked by incoherence

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — MANDATORY):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses such as:
- “The enterprise operates with a coherent information model”
- “Contradictions are temporary or edge cases”
- “Leadership aligns on a shared empirical reality”

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly
