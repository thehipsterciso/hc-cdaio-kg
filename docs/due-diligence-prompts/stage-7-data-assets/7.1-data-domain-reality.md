STAGE 7.1 — DATA DOMAIN REALITY & AUTHORITATIVENESS
(TRUTH OWNERSHIP TEST)
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Enterprise Truth & Authoritativeness Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 7.1 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Produce a data catalog or inventory
- Assume “system of record” equals “system of truth”
- Describe governance frameworks or stewardship models
- Propose consolidation, standardization, or fixes
- Normalize conflict as alignment

Your task is to determine WHICH DATA DOMAINS EXIST IN PRACTICE
and WHICH SYSTEMS ACTUALLY WIN when truth is contested.

If all domains appear uncontested, this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which data domains executives rely on for decisions
- Which systems override others during reconciliation
- Where “official” data is quietly ignored
- Which domains are politically sensitive or routinely disputed
- Who ultimately controls truth when numbers disagree

Outputs must surface EPISTEMIC CONTROL,
not documentation.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material data domains ONLY if they:
- Are referenced in executive decision-making
- Drive financial, operational, or regulatory outcomes
- Are reconciled, restated, or debated repeatedly
- Trigger audit, regulatory, or investor scrutiny
- Become flashpoints during performance discussions

Examples may include (but are not limited to):
- Revenue, bookings, backlog
- Customer counts, churn, retention
- Cost, margin, utilization
- Security, reliability, compliance metrics
- ESG, workforce, or operational KPIs

2. For each domain, identify claimed vs actual truth:
You MUST determine:
- The claimed “system of record”
- The system actually trusted in disputes
- How conflicts are resolved
- Who has authority to declare correctness
- Whether resolution is technical, political, or negotiated

3. Identify contested and sensitive domains:
Explicitly surface:
- Domains with repeated reconciliation effort
- Domains where definitions change by audience
- Domains where leadership avoids specificity
- Domains where silence or ambiguity persists

4. Apply the TRUTH CONFLICT TEST (MANDATORY):
For each domain:
- Describe what happens when numbers disagree
- Identify which system’s output prevails
- Identify who arbitrates disputes
- State what breaks if this domain is challenged publicly

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — MANDATORY):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses such as:
- “Truth is centralized and consistently enforced”
- “Systems of record are systems of truth”
- “Executives rely on the same metrics”

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Financial and operational reporting disclosures
- Earnings call references to metrics and dashboards
- Audit findings tied to data accuracy or reconciliation
- Restatement or correction disclosures
- Litigation or regulatory disputes involving data

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat audit, regulatory, and legal disclosures as primary evidence
- Explicitly identify silent or missing disclosures
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
