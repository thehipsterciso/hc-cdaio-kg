STAGE 7.3 — DATA QUALITY REALITY & ERROR TOLERANCE
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Data Failure & Error Tolerance Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 7.3 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Produce data quality scorecards
- Describe quality programs or tooling
- Assume accuracy is uniformly enforced
- Treat errors as accidental by default
- Propose remediation or controls

Your task is to determine HOW MUCH ERROR
the organization tolerates before acting,
and WHO ACCEPTS THE RISK when data is wrong.

If quality appears uniformly enforced,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which data errors persist chronically
- Which errors trigger escalation vs indifference
- Where decisions proceed despite known inaccuracy
- Where accuracy is sacrificed for speed or convenience
- Who absorbs the consequence of bad data

Outputs must surface ERROR TOLERANCE
AS A FORM OF ORGANIZATIONAL POLICY.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify material data domains ONLY if they:
- Appear in audit, regulatory, or financial disclosures
- Drive customer billing, reporting, or compliance
- Are repeatedly “cleaned,” reconciled, or restated
- Generate disputes with customers, regulators, or auditors
- Are referenced by management as problematic

2. For each domain, identify tolerated vs intolerable error:
You MUST determine:
- Common error types
- Known persistence duration
- The tolerance threshold (explicit or implicit)
- Who accepts the risk when errors persist
- What consequences occur, if any

3. Identify selective quality enforcement:
Explicitly surface:
- Domains where accuracy is non-negotiable
- Domains where “good enough” is acceptable
- Situations where quality enforcement is political
- Tradeoffs between timeliness and correctness

4. Apply the ERROR TOLERANCE TEST (MANDATORY):
For each domain:
- State which errors persist indefinitely
- Identify which errors trigger action
- Identify which stakeholders bear the cost
- State what breaks if zero tolerance is enforced

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — MANDATORY):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses such as:
- “Data accuracy is uniformly enforced”
- “Errors are escalated consistently”
- “Quality failures always block decisions”

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Audit findings tied to data accuracy
- Financial restatement disclosures
- Regulatory actions involving reporting errors
- Customer billing or reporting disputes
- Management commentary on “data cleanup” or reconciliation

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat audit and regulatory findings as primary evidence
- Explicitly identify tolerated errors vs unknown errors
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
