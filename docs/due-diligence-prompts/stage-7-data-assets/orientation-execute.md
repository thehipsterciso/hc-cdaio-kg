STAGE 7 — ORIENTATION
––––––––––––––––––––

STAGE PURPOSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Stage 7 exists to determine how the enterprise
CONSTRUCTS TRUTH, LOSES IT, DISTORTS IT,
AND EXERTS POWER THROUGH INFORMATION.

This stage answers the question:

“When executives argue about reality,
whose data wins — and why?”

It evaluates whether leadership decisions are grounded
in empirical reality, negotiated truth, political convenience,
or institutional habit.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 7 IS:

- A discovery of how the enterprise reasons about itself
- A mapping of epistemic authority, trust, and informational power
- An exposure of where data degrades, mutates, or becomes political
- A test of whether decision-making reflects reality or ritual
- An identification of who controls truth under conflict

Stage 7 focuses on:

- Data domain reality and authoritativeness
- Truth ownership vs “system of record” fiction
- Data lineage, transformation, and semantic drift
- Error tolerance and selective data quality enforcement
- Stewardship, accountability, and blame flow
- Access asymmetry and informational power
- Latency, timeliness, and decision misalignment
- Epistemic coherence vs contradiction at the executive level

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS NOT
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 7 is NOT:

- A data catalog or inventory
- A governance maturity assessment
- A tooling or platform review
- A data strategy precursor
- An analytics capability evaluation
- A metrics framework design exercise

If this stage could be mistaken for
“data governance,” “data management,” or “analytics maturity,”
it has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
STRUCTURE OF STAGE 7 (NO EXECUTION)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 7 will be executed through the following atomic sub-stages:

7.1 Data Domain Reality & Authoritativeness  
→ Which systems are actually trusted when truth is contested

7.2 Data Lineage, Transformation, and Meaning Drift  
→ Where data changes meaning and decisions degrade as a result

7.3 Data Quality Reality & Error Tolerance  
→ Which errors persist, which trigger action, and why

7.4 Data Stewardship, Accountability, and Blame Flow  
→ Who owns data only when it fails

7.5 Data Access, Privilege, and Informational Asymmetry  
→ How visibility shapes power and distorts oversight

7.6 Data Flow Latency, Timeliness, and Decision Misalignment  
→ Whether decisions are made on reality or stale approximation

7.7 Epistemic Coherence & Information System Contradictions  
→ Whether the enterprise survives through contradiction and exception

Each sub-stage:
- Will be hypothesis-driven
- Will require disconfirming evidence searches
- Will preserve uncertainty explicitly
- Will map power, not process
- Will emit multi-file JSON outputs to prevent volume failure

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 7 FAILURE CONDITIONS (GLOBAL)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 7 has FAILED if:

- All data domains appear uncontested
- “System of record” is treated as “system of truth”
- Data quality is described as uniformly enforced
- Accountability aligns neatly with org charts
- Access is described without power implication
- Latency is treated as a technical inconvenience
- Narrative replaces epistemic control analysis

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 7 EXIT CRITERIA (PRE-DECLARED)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 7 will FAIL unless, upon validation:

1. At least 2 contested or politically sensitive data domains are identified  
2. At least 3 data transformations are shown to materially change meaning  
3. At least 1 chronic data error is tolerated due to convenience or power  
4. At least 1 epistemic gatekeeper is identified  
5. At least 1 contradiction in executive “truth” is explicitly documented
