STAGE 7.6 — DATA FLOW LATENCY, TIMELINESS, AND DECISION MISALIGNMENT
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Decision Latency & Timeliness Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 7.6 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Describe reporting calendars generically
- Assume timeliness is acceptable because cadence exists
- Treat latency as a technical inconvenience
- Propose process or tooling changes
- Normalize lag as unavoidable

Your task is to determine WHETHER DECISIONS
ARE MADE ON CURRENT REALITY OR STALE FICTIONS.

If latency is described without decision distortion,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- The lag between real-world events and executive visibility
- Where decisions are made before data stabilizes
- Where timeliness is sacrificed for precision (or vice versa)
- Where workarounds replace authoritative data
- Where latency masks accumulating risk

Outputs must surface DECISION DISTORTION CAUSED BY TIME.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify decision contexts ONLY if they:
- Affect capital allocation, risk posture, or operations
- Are referenced in executive or board materials
- Are subject to recurring revision or reversal
- Rely on lagging or proxy indicators
- Are time-sensitive by nature

2. For each decision context, map latency reality:
You MUST identify:
- The event or condition being decided upon
- The data actually used
- The delay between event and visibility
- Whether data is preliminary, stale, or averaged
- Known workarounds for delayed data

3. Identify decision misalignment:
Explicitly surface:
- Decisions made before data is complete
- Situations where speed overrides accuracy
- Situations where accuracy arrives too late to matter
- Areas where leaders knowingly operate on approximations

4. Apply the LATENCY DISTORTION TEST (MANDATORY):
For each context:
- State how latency changes the decision taken
- Identify risks masked by delay
- Identify risks created by premature action
- State what breaks if latency increases further

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — MANDATORY):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses such as:
- “Decisions are made on near-real-time data”
- “Latency does not materially distort outcomes”
- “Timeliness tradeoffs are consciously managed”

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Reporting cadence disclosures
- Earnings call references to lagging indicators

- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy

- Explicitly identify silence or absence of timing disclosure
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
