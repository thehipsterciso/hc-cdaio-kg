STAGE 5.6 — FINANCIAL COHERENCE & INTERNAL ECONOMIC CONTRADICTIONS
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Financial Integrity & Coherence Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 5.6 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Normalize or rationalize contradictions
- Treat accounting adjustments as economic resolution
- Assume contradictions are temporary or transitional
- Propose remediation, restructuring, or optimization
- Collapse tension into narrative coherence

Your task is to determine whether the financial model is
ECONOMICALLY COHERENT or sustained through STRUCTURAL CONTRADICTION.

If the model is labeled “coherent” without identifying tension,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where reported financial success undermines operating reality
- Where cost discipline destroys revenue durability
- Where growth mechanics degrade margins or cash
- Where incentives misalign with economic truth
- Where accounting artifacts mask economic decay
- Whether the model requires continuous restructuring to appear viable

Outputs must surface FINANCIAL CONTRADICTIONS
that create fragility under stress.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify internal financial contradictions:
Surface situations where:
- Growth increases revenue but destroys margin or cash
- Cost reductions impair revenue durability
- Margin expansion relies on capability starvation
- Financial incentives conflict with operating constraints
- Reported improvement worsens economic survivability

2. Map contradiction mechanics:
For each contradiction, identify:
- The financial behavior or metric involved
- The operational or economic counter-effect
- Why the contradiction persists
- Who benefits from maintaining it
- What stress condition exposes it

3. Identify accounting or structural masking:
Explicitly surface:
- Reclassification, capitalization, or adjustment practices
- “One-time” items that recur
- Restructuring used as a margin support mechanism
- Deferred costs that resurface later

4. Apply the STRESS TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each contradiction:
- State what breaks if the contradiction is forced to resolve
- Identify whether failure is ECONOMIC, LIQUIDITY, or CONTROL-based
- Identify whether impact is immediate or delayed

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Financial coherence vs contradiction
- Dependence on continuous restructuring
- Misalignment between reported success and economic health

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Cross-stage outputs from 5.1–5.5
- Margin, cost, liquidity, and capital allocation disclosures

You MUST:

- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
