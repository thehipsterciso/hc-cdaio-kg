STAGE 5 — ORIENTATION
––––––––––––––––––––

STAGE PURPOSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Stage 5 exists to determine the FINANCIAL REALITY of the enterprise
under stress, constraint, and downside conditions.

This stage answers the question:

“When money becomes scarce or constrained,
who gains control, what collapses first,
and what becomes impossible regardless of intent?”

It evaluates whether the enterprise uncovered in Stages 1–4
is FINANCIALLY SURVIVABLE, OPERABLE, AND CONTROLLABLE
when conditions deteriorate.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 5 IS:

- A test of economic survivability under pressure
- A mapping of financial control dynamics under stress
- A separation of reported performance from usable capital
- A determination of downside boundaries and failure order
- An analysis of who controls decisions when capital tightens
- A stress-oriented view of revenue, cost, liquidity, and control

Stage 5 focuses on:

- Revenue durability and quality under pressure
- Margin reality and cost physics under stress
- Liquidity accessibility and cash control
- Debt, covenants, and control transfer mechanics
- Capital allocation reality and change capacity
- Internal financial contradictions and coherence

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT THIS STAGE IS NOT
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 5 is NOT:

- A financial statement walkthrough
- A ratio or multiple analysis
- A valuation or pricing exercise
- A forecasting or budgeting model
- An earnings call recap
- A performance narrative or investor presentation

If this stage could be mistaken for an earnings review
or a valuation analysis,
it has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
STRUCTURE OF STAGE 5 (NO EXECUTION)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 5 will be executed through the following atomic sub-stages:

5.1 Revenue Quality, Stability, and Signal Integrity  
→ Which revenue survives pressure and which collapses first

5.2 Margin Reality, Cost Physics, and Operating Leverage  
→ How costs and margins behave when volume, price, or mix deteriorates

5.3 Liquidity Reality, Cash Accessibility, and Runway  
→ How much cash is actually usable, by whom, and for how long

5.4 Debt, Covenants, and Control Transfer Mechanics  
→ When and how financial control shifts away from management

5.5 Capital Allocation Reality & Change Capacity  
→ Whether the enterprise can fund change without destabilization

5.6 Financial Coherence & Internal Economic Contradictions  
→ Where the financial model sustains itself through contradiction

Each sub-stage:
- Will be hypothesis-driven
- Will require disconfirming evidence searches
- Will apply downside and stress “what breaks first” tests
- Will preserve uncertainty explicitly
- Will emit multi-file JSON outputs to prevent volume failure

––––––––––––––––––––
STAGE 5 FAILURE CONDITIONS (GLOBAL)
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 5 has FAILED if:

- Revenue is discussed without identifying failure modes
- Margins are analyzed without stress behavior
- Liquidity is treated as a balance rather than control
- Debt is discussed without mapping control transfer
- Capital allocation is inferred from intent rather than cash reality
- Financial contradictions are smoothed or ignored
- Narrative replaces downside boundary definition
