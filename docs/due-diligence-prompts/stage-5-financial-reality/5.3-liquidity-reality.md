STAGE 5.3 — LIQUIDITY REALITY, CASH ACCESSIBILITY, AND RUNWAY
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Liquidity & Cash Control Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 5.3 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Treat reported cash balances as usable cash
- Assume intercompany cash mobility
- Perform cash forecasting models
- Infer liquidity strength from historical performance
- Conflate accounting liquidity with operational control

Your task is to determine HOW MUCH CASH IS ACTUALLY USABLE,
BY WHOM, AND UNDER WHAT CONDITIONS.

If liquidity is discussed without addressing cash control,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- How much reported cash is actually accessible
- Where cash is restricted, trapped, or encumbered
- Who controls cash release decisions
- How long the business can operate under downside stress
- Which expenses cannot be deferred or avoided

Outputs must surface CASH FAILURE MODES
and LIQUIDITY CONTROL POINTS,
not balance sheet comfort.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Decompose reported liquidity:
Identify, where disclosed:
- Total reported cash and equivalents
- Restricted vs unrestricted cash
- Jurisdictional or entity-level cash traps
- Cash pledged, reserved, or collateralized
- Intercompany transfer limitations

2. Map cash control mechanics:
For each major cash pool, identify:
- Legal owner and controlling entity
- Covenant, regulatory, or contractual restrictions
- Approval mechanisms for cash use
- Conditions under which access tightens

3. Assess cash burn under stress:
Explicitly surface:
- Core operating burn that cannot be deferred
- Obligations that accelerate under stress
- Lag between revenue decline and cash impact
- Liquidity sensitivity to covenant pressure

4. Apply the STRESS TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For liquidity as a system:
- State what happens if revenue declines materially
- State what happens if capital markets close
- Identify first liquidity failure point
- Identify timeline to operational impairment

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Usable cash being materially lower than reported
- Cash control shifting under stress
- Liquidity failure preceding solvency failure

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Cash flow statements
- Liquidity and capital resources disclosures
- Debt agreements and covenant summaries
- Subsidiary cash and repatriation disclosures
- Management commentary on liquidity
- Auditor emphasis-of-matter language (if any)

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat usability estimates as inference unless explicitly disclosed
- Explicitly identify missing or opaque disclosures
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
