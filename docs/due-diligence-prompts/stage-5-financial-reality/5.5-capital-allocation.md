STAGE 5.5 — CAPITAL ALLOCATION REALITY & CHANGE CAPACITY
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Capital Allocation & Change Capacity Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 5.5 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Assume capital follows stated strategy or priorities
- Treat approved budgets as funded capability
- Infer change capacity from intent or leadership rhetoric
- Propose investment, divestment, or reprioritization actions
- Evaluate “ROI” or business cases

Your task is to determine WHETHER THE ENTERPRISE CAN FUND CHANGE
WITHOUT DESTABILIZING CORE OPERATIONS OR VIOLATING CONSTRAINTS.

If change capacity is inferred from intent rather than capital reality,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- How much capital is truly discretionary
- Which spending is politically, contractually, or structurally protected
- Where survival spending crowds out improvement
- Where chronic underinvestment has accumulated
- Which change initiatives failed due to capital starvation
- Where capital competition creates execution paralysis

Outputs must surface CAPITAL FAILURE MODES
and CHANGE CAPACITY LIMITS,
not budget narratives.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Decompose capital allocation reality:
Identify, where disclosed:
- Capex vs opex composition
- Maintenance vs growth vs compliance spend
- Recurrent “one-time” restructuring or transformation costs
- Spend tied to regulatory, contractual, or survival requirements

2. Identify discretionary vs protected spend:
For major spend categories, identify:
- Truly discretionary capital
- Spend protected by contracts, politics, or covenants
- Spend that is deferred but not eliminated
- Spend that cannot be cut without triggering failure elsewhere

3. Surface underinvestment and deferred maintenance:
Explicitly identify:
- Repeated deferral of infrastructure, platforms, or systems
- Backlogs created by cost control
- Signals of “starve to survive” behavior
- Accumulation of latent execution risk

4. Apply the STRESS TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For capital allocation as a system:
- State what gets cut first under stress
- State what never gets cut regardless of performance
- Identify where cuts create cascading failures
- Identify which change efforts fail first due to funding withdrawal

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Change capacity being materially lower than stated
- Capital allocation reinforcing status quo fragility
- Survival spend crowding out improvement

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Capex vs opex disclosures
- Investment vs maintenance commentary
- Restructuring and transformation charge disclosures
- Deferred maintenance or technical debt references
- Program cancellation or delay disclosures
- Management commentary on capital discipline

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat change-capacity judgments explicitly as inference
- Explicitly identify missing or opaque disclosures
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or resolve gaps
