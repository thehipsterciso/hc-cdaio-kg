# STAGE 3 — MARKET REALITY, EXTERNAL PRESSURE, AND COMPETITIVE FORCE



Purpose: Determine how external forces constrain revenue durability, margins, control, and optionality
Standard: M&A market diligence + adversarial competitive analysis
Output: External pressure map, not positioning narrative

“How does the world push back on this business—and where does that pressure become decisive?”

3.0 Stage Framing (MANDATORY)

What this stage IS

An analysis of forces acting on the company
A determination of where the company has agency vs exposure
A test of whether the business model survives external pressure
A separation of storytelling from survivability

What this stage IS NOT

A market overview
A TAM/SAM/SOM exercise
A competitor feature comparison
A “leader / challenger” quadrant discussion

If this stage could be mistaken for analyst research, it has failed.

3.1 Market Structure, Power Distribution, and Value Migration

Agent Role: Market Structure & Power Analyst

Mission (Non-Negotiable)

Determine who has power in the market and where economic value is migrating over time.

You are not describing market size.
You are mapping power, leverage, and inevitability.

Analytical Burden

You must prove:

Where pricing power resides
Which players dictate terms
Where margins structurally compress or expand
Where value is migrating away from the company’s role

Mandatory Evidence

Pricing trend disclosures
Gross margin trends across peers
Hyperscaler / platform dominance signals
Industry consolidation patterns
Analyst downgrade rationales (secondary, labeled)
Public procurement behavior (if relevant)

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

Who sets prices in this market?
Who absorbs margin compression?
Where has value moved in the last 5–10 years?
Which roles in the value chain are becoming interchangeable?

3.2 Competitive Landscape as Threat Vectors (Not Peer Lists)

Agent Role: Competitive Threat & Asymmetry Analyst

Mission

Determine which competitors can actually hurt the business, how, and under what conditions.

You are not cataloging competitors.
You are identifying credible threat vectors.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

Competitors that can undercut pricing sustainably
Competitors that can displace via bundling or platform leverage
Competitors that change customer expectations
Competitors that weaponize scale or regulation

Mandatory Evidence

Win/loss commentary
Pricing pressure disclosures
Partner displacement narratives
Product bundling disclosures
Customer switching behavior (where public)
Litigation or antitrust actions affecting competitors

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Which competitor could destroy margins without stealing customers?
Which competitor could steal customers without competing on features?
Which competitor benefits from regulation?
Which competitor benefits from commoditization?

3.3 Customer Buying Behavior, Switching Physics, and Inertia

Agent Role: Customer Behavior & Switching Cost Analyst

Mission

Determine why customers stay, leave, or tolerate dissatisfaction.

This is not satisfaction analysis.
This is switching physics.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

True switching costs (technical, contractual, organizational)
Customer tolerance thresholds
Situations where inertia masquerades as loyalty
Conditions that trigger rapid churn

Mandatory Evidence

Renewal rate disclosures
Churn commentary
Customer complaint patterns
SLA penalties and disputes
Customer references vs cancellations
Transition costs disclosed in filings or case law

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

What breaks customer inertia?
What event causes mass reconsideration?
Which customers are locked vs choosing?
Where dissatisfaction does not equal churn—yet?

3.4 Pricing Dynamics, Discounting, and Market Signaling

Agent Role: Pricing Power & Signaling Analyst

Mission

Determine what pricing actually signals about market power and desperation.

Analytical Burden

You must surface:

Discounting patterns and escalation
Price protection mechanisms
Concessions tied to renewals or upsells
Silent price erosion via scope creep

Mandatory Evidence

Gross margin trends
Earnings call pricing commentary
Discounting disclosures
Contract renegotiation disclosures
Competitive pricing references

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Where price is used to mask execution weakness
Where price concessions substitute for differentiation
Where pricing power exists but is unused
Where price signals desperation to the market

3.5 Partner, Platform, and Channel Dependency as Market Force

Mission

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

Channel control points
Platform gatekeeping behaviors
Co-sell asymmetries
Channel-driven margin compression
Disintermediation risk

Mandatory Evidence

Partner program disclosures
Co-sell announcements
Platform policy changes
Revenue attribution shifts
Margin commentary tied to partner mix

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Who controls customer access?
Who controls pricing narratives?
Who can change rules unilaterally?
Who absorbs margin when things tighten?

3.6 External Shock Sensitivity & Market Stress Response

Agent Role: Market Shock & Stress Analyst

Mission

Determine how the business behaves under macro, regulatory, or technological shock.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

Revenue sensitivity to macro downturns
Exposure to regulatory shifts
Sensitivity to technology disruption
Lag between shock and financial impact

Mandatory Evidence

Performance during prior downturns
Management commentary during stress periods
Sector-wide shock impacts
Peer failure or consolidation events

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

What shock causes permanent damage?
What shock merely delays revenue?
What shock accelerates commoditization?
What shock transfers power to others?

Stage 3 Exit Criteria (ABSOLUTE)

Stage 3 is FAILED unless:

At least 3 external pressure forces are identified and evidenced
At least 2 power asymmetries unfavorable to the company are documented
At least 1 competitive threat is shown to damage economics without head-to-head competition
At least 1 false belief about customer loyalty or pricing power is invalidated
At least 1 external shock is shown to create irreversible damage or power transfer
