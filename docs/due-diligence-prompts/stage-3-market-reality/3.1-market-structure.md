STAGE 3.1 — MARKET STRUCTURE, POWER DISTRIBUTION, AND VALUE MIGRATION
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Market Structure & Power Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 3.1 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Produce a market overview or size narrative
- Perform TAM/SAM/SOM analysis
- Compare competitors feature-by-feature
- Treat all market participants as symmetric
- Assume pricing power without evidence
- Drift into analyst-style positioning language

Your task is to determine WHO HAS POWER,
HOW THAT POWER IS EXERCISED,
and WHERE ECONOMIC VALUE IS MIGRATING OVER TIME.

If you cannot identify who structurally wins and who structurally loses,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Where pricing power resides in the value chain
- Which actors dictate commercial or technical terms
- Which roles are becoming commoditized or interchangeable
- Where margins structurally compress or expand
- How value has migrated over the last 5–10 years
- Where the enterprise’s role is gaining or losing inevitability

Outputs must surface MARKET POWER REALITY,
not competitive storytelling.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Map market structure and power distribution:
Identify distinct roles in the value chain and determine:
- Which actors control pricing, access, standards, or switching
- How power is exercised (scale, bundling, control points, regulation)
- Which actors absorb margin pressure vs pass it through
- Which actors can unilaterally change terms

2. Identify value migration patterns:
Explicitly surface:
- Where value has moved away from historically (e.g., services → platforms)
- Where margins have structurally compressed
- Where margins have expanded or consolidated
- Which roles are becoming interchangeable or low-power

3. Identify inevitable margin pressures:
Surface margin pressure ONLY if:
- It is structural, not cyclical
- It is observable across peers or over time
- It results from power asymmetry or commoditization

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each major power dynamic:
- State what breaks if the enterprise’s role is weakened
- Identify ECONOMIC, OPERATIONAL, or CONTROL breakage
- Identify whether impact is localized or systemic

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Where pricing power resides
- Whether the enterprise is a price taker or price setter
- Whether value migration is reversible or irreversible

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Pricing trend disclosures
- Gross margin trends (company and peers)
- Hyperscaler or platform dominance signals
- Industry consolidation or vertical integration patterns
- Public procurement behavior (if relevant)
- Analyst downgrade rationales (SECONDARY, clearly labeled)

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Explicitly flag inference vs primary evidence
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or narratively resolve gaps
