STAGE 3.2 — COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE AS THREAT VECTORS
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Competitive Threat & Asymmetry Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 3.2 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Produce a competitor list or peer comparison
- Treat competitors as symmetric
- Evaluate feature parity or product roadmaps
- Assume competitive impact requires customer switching
- Drift into market positioning or strategy

Your task is to determine WHICH COMPETITORS CAN
MATERIALLY DAMAGE THE BUSINESS,
HOW THEY DO SO,
AND UNDER WHAT CONDITIONS.

If all competitors are treated as equally relevant,
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- Which competitors can undercut pricing sustainably
- Which competitors can compress margins without stealing customers
- Which competitors can displace via bundling, platforms, or regulation
- Which competitors benefit structurally from commoditization
- Which competitors change customer expectations or buying physics

Outputs must surface COMPETITIVE DAMAGE MECHANISMS,
not rivalry narratives.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Identify credible competitive threat vectors:
Identify competitors ONLY if they:
- Can damage economics without head-to-head competition
- Can impose pricing pressure via scale or bundling
- Can displace value via platform control or ecosystem leverage
- Benefit structurally from regulation, standards, or procurement rules
- Weaponize cost, scope, or access asymmetrically

Competitor types may include:
- Platforms
- Hyperscalers
- Low-cost or offshore providers
- Integrated incumbents
- Regulatory-advantaged actors

2. Map threat vectors to damage mechanisms:
For each threat, identify:
- The mechanism of damage (pricing, bundling, displacement, expectation shift)
- Conditions under which damage becomes material
- Whether damage is immediate, latent, or cascading
- Whether customers must switch for damage to occur

3. Assess company response flexibility:
For each threat vector, assess (directionally):
- Ability to respond via pricing, scope, or contract
- Ability to respond via differentiation (without assuming success)
- Structural constraints that limit response (Stage 1 / Stage 2)

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each threat vector:
- State what breaks if the threat materializes
- Identify ECONOMIC, OPERATIONAL, or CONTROL breakage
- Classify impact as localized or systemic

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Whether competition damages economics without displacement
- Whether competitive threats are concentrated or diffuse
- Whether competitive pressure is accelerating or plateauing

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Win / loss commentary
- Pricing pressure disclosures
- Margin compression commentary
- Partner displacement narratives
- Product bundling disclosures
- Customer switching behavior (where public)
- Litigation or antitrust actions affecting competitors

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Treat competitive inference explicitly as inference
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or narratively resolve gaps
