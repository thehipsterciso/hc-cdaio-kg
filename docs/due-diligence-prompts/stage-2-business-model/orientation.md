# STAGE 2 — BUSINESS MODEL, VALUE CREATION, AND ECONOMIC FLOW REALITY



Purpose: Determine how the enterprise actually makes money, loses money, and sustains itself
Standard: M&A operational diligence + economic truth discovery
Output: Value-creation physics, not business descriptions

2.0 Stage Framing (MANDATORY)

What this stage IS

A decomposition of economic reality
A mapping of where value is created vs where it is reported
An identification of cross-subsidies, fragility, and illusion
A test of whether the business model is internally coherent

What this stage IS NOT

A summary of segments
A restatement of revenue lines
A market positioning exercise
A growth narrative

If the output could be mistaken for an investor deck, this stage has failed.

2.1 Revenue Architecture & Monetization Truth

Agent Role: Revenue Mechanics & Monetization Analyst

Mission (Non-Negotiable)

Determine what customers actually pay for, under what conditions, and why revenue persists or disappears.

You are not cataloging revenue streams.
You are testing the durability and mechanics of monetization.

Analytical Burden

You must prove:

What triggers revenue recognition in practice
What customer behavior actually sustains revenue
Which revenue is contractual vs contingent
Which revenue is repeatable vs situational
Where revenue is propped up by concessions or bundling

Mandatory Evidence

Revenue recognition disclosures
Contract type disclosures
Earnings call Q&A (language analysis required)
Customer case studies (marketing vs reality)
Pricing and discounting commentary
Deferred revenue disclosures

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

What happens to revenue if this customer behavior changes?
Which revenue streams collapse without cross-sell or bundling?
Which services are sold at a loss to preserve logos?
Which revenues exist only because exit costs are high?

2.2 Unit Economics, Cost Physics, and Margin Reality

Agent Role: Unit Economics & Margin Integrity Analyst

Mission

Determine whether economic value is created at the unit level — or merely aggregated into existence.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

True unit of economic activity (not reported unit)
Variable vs fixed cost realities
Margin compression drivers
Cost displacement between LoBs
Structural loss leaders

Mandatory Evidence

Segment margin disclosures
Cost-of-revenue disclosures
Labor mix disclosures
Cloud / data center cost commentary
Partner revenue share disclosures
Restructuring charges and “one-time” costs

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Which units lose money individually?
Where margins improve only via accounting or reclassification?
Which costs scale superlinearly?
Where growth worsens margins?

2.3 End-to-End Value Flow & Friction Mapping

Agent Role: Value Flow & Friction Analyst

Mission

Map how value moves through the enterprise — and where it degrades, leaks, or stalls.

Analytical Burden

You must prove:

Where value is created vs where it is captured
Where internal handoffs destroy margin
Where rework or delay erodes economics
Where organizational boundaries fragment accountability

Mandatory Evidence

Order-to-cash descriptions
Delivery model disclosures
Customer complaint patterns
SLA / penalty disclosures
Earnings call commentary on execution issues

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Where value changes hands internally
Where no one owns outcomes
Where process complexity is monetized against the company
Where scale increases friction rather than reducing it

2.4 Customer Segmentation Reality & Economic Dependence

Agent Role: Customer Economics & Concentration Analyst

Mission

Determine which customers actually matter economically, not by logo count.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

Revenue concentration vs margin concentration
Customers that are economically toxic
Customers that subsidize others
Customers that exert pricing or operational control

Mandatory Evidence

Customer concentration disclosures
Vertical segmentation
Renewal and churn commentary
Contract duration disclosures
Pricing concession commentary

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Which customers would you drop if free to choose?
Which customers anchor the cost structure?
Which customers block operational change?
Which customers distort reported growth?

2.5 Partner, Channel, and Value Capture Asymmetry

Agent Role: Partner Economics & Power Analyst

Mission

Determine who captures value in the ecosystem — the company or its partners.

Analytical Burden

You must surface:

Revenue share asymmetries
Cost externalization onto the company
Partner-driven pricing pressure
Partner exit or repricing risk

Mandatory Evidence

Partner program disclosures
Hyperscaler relationship disclosures
Co-sell and resale terms (where public)
Earnings call commentary on partners
Margin commentary tied to partner mix

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly test:

Who has pricing power?
Who bears operational cost?
Who exits with least damage?
Where “strategic partnership” masks dependency?

2.6 Business Model Coherence & Internal Contradictions

Agent Role: Business Model Integrity Analyst

Mission

Determine whether the business model is internally consistent — or survives only through contradiction.

Analytical Burden

You must identify:

Conflicting incentives between LoBs
Growth that undermines profitability
Sales promises that operations cannot fulfill
Structural tension between scale and quality

Mandatory Adversarial Tests

Explicitly answer:

Where does success in one area cause failure elsewhere?
Where does growth increase fragility?
Where does the model rely on customer inertia?
Where does reported performance contradict operational reality?
