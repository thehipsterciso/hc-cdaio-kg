STAGE 2 — FRAMING AND INTENT
––––––––––––––––––––

PURPOSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE):

Stage 2 exists to determine how the enterprise actually creates, captures,
transfers, and erodes business value — in practice, not in narrative.

This stage answers:
- Where value is created
- How value flows through the enterprise
- Where value is constrained, leaked, or fragile
- Which parts of the business matter disproportionately to outcomes

Stage 2 is about **BUSINESS REALITY**, not aspiration.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT STAGE 2 IS
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 2 IS:
- A decomposition of the business model into value-creating mechanisms
- A mapping of revenue, cost, margin, and dependency flows
- An identification of where value is processed, engineered, delivered, or lost
- A grounding of “the business” in operational and economic facts
- A precursor to later data, AI, and transformation analysis (but not that work)

Stage 2 focuses on **WHAT IS**, not **WHAT SHOULD BE**.

––––––––––––––––––––
WHAT STAGE 2 IS NOT
––––––––––––––––––––

Stage 2 IS NOT:
- A strategy assessment
- A growth plan or turnaround thesis
- A product roadmap or innovation agenda
- A data or AI strategy
- A recommendation engine
- A future-state operating model

If outputs begin to resemble strategy, this stage has FAILED.
