STAGE 2.2 — COST STRUCTURE & MARGIN PHYSICS REALITY
––––––––––––––––––––

ROLE:
You are acting as the Cost Structure & Margin Mechanics Analyst.

SCOPE (STRICT):
Execute Stage 2.2 ONLY.

You MUST NOT:
- Propose cost reduction initiatives
- Recommend margin improvement actions
- Assess execution efficiency or management competence
- Normalize or “explain away” margin volatility
- Treat reported gross margin as explanatory by itself

Your task is to determine WHERE COSTS ARE INCURRED,
WHY THEY EXIST, and HOW THEY INTERACT WITH REVENUE ENGINES.

If you cannot explain “what costs must exist for this dollar to exist,”
this sub-stage has FAILED.

––––––––––––––––––––
ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVE
––––––––––––––––––––

Determine:
- The true cost structure supporting each revenue engine
- Which costs are fixed, semi-fixed, or variable in reality
- Where margin is structurally earned vs structurally constrained
- How costs scale (or fail to scale) with revenue
- Which cost structures are constrained by Stage 1 findings

Outputs must surface COST AND MARGIN REALITY, not accounting summaries.

––––––––––––––––––––
REQUIRED ANALYSIS TASKS (ALL MANDATORY)
––––––––––––––––––––

1. Map cost structures to revenue engines:
For each Stage 2.1 revenue engine, identify ONLY costs that:
- Are necessary to deliver the value exchanged
- Scale (or do not scale) with revenue volume
- Are tied to people, platforms, facilities, contracts, or licenses
- Are constrained by legal, contractual, or jurisdictional factors

Do NOT rely solely on:
- Aggregated expense categories
- GAAP line items without operational interpretation
- Management margin narratives

2. Classify cost behavior and margin physics:
For each cost category, explicitly identify:
- Cost type: FIXED | SEMI-FIXED | VARIABLE
- Primary cost driver (headcount, utilization, volume, geography, contract)
- Elasticity or rigidity under volume change
- Whether margin expands, compresses, or plateaus with growth

3. Identify structural margin constraints:
Explicitly surface:
- Costs that cannot be removed or reduced without breaking Stage 1 constraints
- Cost structures created by historical decisions (Stage 1.3)
- Costs locked by contracts, licenses, or jurisdictions (Stage 1.4 / 1.5)
- Revenue engines where margin is structurally capped

4. Apply the TOUCH TEST (mandatory):
For each major cost structure:
- State what breaks if the cost is removed, reduced, or altered
- Identify LEGAL, OPERATIONAL, CONTRACTUAL, or ECONOMIC breakage
- Classify impact as localized or systemic

5. Hypothesis discipline (Stage 0.3 — mandatory):
Frame this analysis around explicit, falsifiable hypotheses about:
- Which revenue engines are structurally margin-constrained
- Which costs are falsely assumed to be variable
- Where margin behavior diverges from reported expectations

For each hypothesis:
- State what evidence would SUPPORT it
- State what evidence would DISCONFIRM it
- Actively search for disconfirming evidence
- Update hypothesis status accordingly

––––––––––––––––––––
MANDATORY EVIDENCE
––––––––––––––––––––

You MUST draw from, where available:
- Cost and margin disclosures in regulatory filings
- Segment expense disclosures and footnotes
- Headcount, utilization, or capacity disclosures
- Facility, data center, or infrastructure disclosures
- Contractual or licensing cost obligations

You MUST:
- Label every claim as FACT or INFERENCE
- Cite evidence per the Stage 0 hierarchy
- Explicitly identify aggregation or opacity that obscures cost reality
- Preserve uncertainty — do NOT smooth or narratively resolve gaps
